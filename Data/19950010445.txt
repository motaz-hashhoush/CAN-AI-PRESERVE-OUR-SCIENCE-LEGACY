ABSTRACT\n\n(Maximum\n\n200 words)\n\nIn an effort to determine an optimum method for ranking the fracture toughness of developmental aluminum\nalloys for High Speed Civil Transport applications, five labs performed K and/or J based fracture tests on\naluminum alloy 2024-T3. Two material thicknesses were examined: 0.063 in. and 0.125 in, Middle crack\ntension and compact tension specimens were excised from 60 in, wide middle crack tension panels which had\nbeen previously tested at Boeing. The crack resistance curves generated were compared to the R-curves from\n60 in. wide specimens. The experimental program indicated that effective stress intensity from secant\ncompliance based crack length and stress intensity calculated from J-integral testing were equivalent. In\naddition, comparison of defferent specimen sezes and configurations indicated that standard validity\nrequirements for compact tension specimens may be overly restrictive.\n\n14.
