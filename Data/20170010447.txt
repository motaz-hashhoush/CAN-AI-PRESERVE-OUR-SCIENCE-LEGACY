The amount of water in the helmet subsequently\nincreased and began to collect on his face.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Water began to cover his eyes, nose, ears, and communications cap.\nLuca experienced impaired breathing, vision, communication and\nhearing impeding communication. Over 1.5 liters accumulated in the\nhelmet.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 The EVA was emergently terminated and the crew made their way\nback to the airlock.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Though ultimately Parmitano fared well, aspiration of water could\nhave required his crewmates to perform an emergency\nresuscitation.\n\nMishap Board Findings\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Inorganic materials caused blockage of the drum holes in the EMU\n(Extravehicular Mobility Unit) water separator resulting in water\nspilling into the vent loop that feeds into the helmet.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 NASA team had a lack of knowledge regarding this particular failure\nmode.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Similar suit failure misdiagnosis when it initially occurred on a\nprevious EVA.\n\nLessons Learned from Water in Helmet\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Complex systems carry unknown/\nunrealized hazards\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Creative Solutions to mitigate risks\n- Helmet Absorption Pad (HAP)\n- Procedure Updates\n- Hand Signals\n- Snorkel\n\nNASA\n\nNASA\n\nSummary\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Best plans and training for complex operations should also include\ncontingency situations.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Continual evaluation of complex operations is required in order to\nimprove safety, procedures and other products required for\nexecution.\n\nNASA\n\nNASA\n\nQuestions?\n\nAsMA\n\nDowntown Denver\n\nDenver\nNASA\n\nNASA\n\n'
