Project Objectives\n\xe2\x80\x93 Provide the foundational knowledge required to qualify the Selective Laser Melting (SLM)\nprocess for structural hardware in spaceflight applications.\n\xe2\x80\x93 Validate the qualification and certification methodology presented in the MSFC draft, Engineering\nand Quality Standard for Additively Manufactured Spaceflight Hardware.\n\xe2\x80\xa2\nParticipants\n\xe2\x80\x93 ARC, GRC, LaRC, MSFC, NASA NDE Working Group (NNWG)\n\xe2\x80\xa2\nTasks\n1. Establish feedstock controls and maximum recycle limits for Alloy 718 powder. Identify powder\ncontrol metrics for inclusion in standard.\n2. Apply statistical process controls to powder fusion systems. Evaluate methodology at\ncommercial vendors having mature SLM alloy 718 processes\n3. Derive statistically-based design allowables for SLM alloy 718 and compare with traditional Abasis methodology. Publish design allowables for SLM alloy 718 based on a minimum five\npowder lots.\n4. Design and build analogue engine articles to evaluate platform-to-platform variability; establish\n\xe2\x80\x9cfirst article\xe2\x80\x9d inspection methodology; and interrogate critical features.\n5. Characterize defect structures of SLM alloy 718 material built within and at the limits of the\nprocess window. Provide test articles to NNWG for NDE studies.
