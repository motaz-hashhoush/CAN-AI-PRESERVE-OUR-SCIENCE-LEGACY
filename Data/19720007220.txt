Both flight and control samples were evaluated.\nSome conclusions resulting from a comparison of the flight and control samples a r e as follows:\nI Solidification in neither the flight nor control samples was truly directional.\n.\n2. Apparent intermittent contact of the melt with the container in the flight samples led to\n\nunusual nucleation and growth structures.\n3. There was greater uniformity, on a macro scale, of both pores and structural features in\nthe flight sample; presumably the result of the reduced gravity conditions.\n4. It seems quite feasible to produce enhanced dispersions of gases and dense phases in a melt\nwhich is solidified in reduced gravity.\n5. A two-stage heating/cooling cycle may help directional solidification.\n6.
