Geoidal undo ations of small horizontal scales but ranging in amplitude from one to four meters appa\xe2\x80\x9ear poorly modelled and\ncaused inaccurate results in the DH. For example, if a geoidal undulation happens to\n\ncover an area within the 30\' grid it can very well be that the interpolated geoid heights\nover such area will differ by as much as four meters from the altimeter reading. There is\nlittle doubt that such large excursions in the residuals are caused by the lick of geoidal\n\nresolution.\nAnother problem associated with the variation maps is that the mean surface topography is greatly biased by the large number of passes in the last few months of the study.\nAn inspection of a frequency distribution of passes ( Figure 9) leaves no doubt that Summer\nand Fall 1978 carry very heavy weights in a mean surface topography. Furthermore seasonal\n\nmaps obtained from just a few passes contain large areas of interpolated results which\nshould be viewed with deep reservations. Fortunately these interpolated areas can be\nreadily noticed since the contour lines appear as almost straight lines. For example,\ncoverage of Sumner 1977 shows there is a large area in the western middle Gulf of Mexico\ncentered around 27 \xc2\xb0 N, 261\xc2\xb0E where there are no altimeter passes, the t ypography for that\n\nsame season shows the distinct straight lines indicative of the automatic interpolation\nwhich is performed to fill in grid squares void of values. Yet the variations shown on\nthat some map for the Eastern Gulf are reliable since there is a good distribution of\npasses.\nAnalysis of the altimeter coverage for the fifteen seasonal topographic variation\nmaps (see Figures 10, 11, 12, and 13) reveal that the first two maps are totally unreliable because of poor coverage. Eight other seasons show fairly good coverage for the\nEastern Gulf but poorly covered in the Western sectors. The remaining five seasonal maps\nshow excellent coverage. It is important to note that only the very major Features can\nbe obtained; many smaller variations may be due to ocean dynamics but the results are\n\ninconclusive at best. The probability that features in excess of 150 cm shown in these\n5\n\ns\n\nWraps are caused by dynamic features in the Gulf of Mexico is high.
