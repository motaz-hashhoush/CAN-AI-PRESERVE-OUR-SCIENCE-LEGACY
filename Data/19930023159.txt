This technique belongs in the feamwJobject-based family.\nThe motion and shape of a small window, assumed to be fully contained inside the boundaries of some object, is\napproximated by an affine transformation. The parameters of the transformation matrix are derived by initially comparing\nsuccessive images, and progressively increasing the image time separation so as to achieve much largertriangulation baseline\nthan currently possible. Depth is directly derived from the expansion part of the transformation.\nTo a first approximation, image-plane expansion is independent of image-plane location with respect to the focus of\nexpansion (FOE) and of platform maneuvers. Thus, an expansion-based method has the potential of providing a reliable range\nin the difficult image area around the FOE. In areas farfrom the FOE the shift parameters of the affine transformationcan provide\nmore accurate depth information than the expansion alone, and can thus be used similarly to the way they have been used in\nconjunction with the Inertial Navigation Unit (INU) and Kalman filtering. However, the performance of a shift-based algorithm,\nwhen the shifts are derived from the affine wansformation, would be much improved compared to current algorithms because\nthe shifts--as well as the otherparameters--can be obtainedbetween widely separated images.\nThus, the main advantage of this new approach is that, allowing the tracked window to expand and rotate, in addition to\nmoving laterally,enables one to correlate images over a very long time span which, inturn, translates into a large spatialbaseline--resulting in a proportionately higher depth accuracy.\nSUBJECT\n\n15.\n\nTERMS\n\nExpansive-based\n\npassive ranging,\n\nImage processing,\n\nNUMBER\n\n16.\n\n14.\n\nOF PAGES\n\nPRICE\n\n20.\n\nLIMITATION\n\n61\n\nOptical flow\n\nCODE\n\nA04\n17.\n\nSECURITY\nCLASSIFICATION\nOF REPORT\n\nUnclassified\nNSN 7540.01-280-5500\n\n18.\n\nSECURITY\nCLASSIFICATION\nOF THIS PAGE\n\n19.\n\nSECURITY\nCLASSIFICATION\nOF ABSTRACT\n\nOF ABSTRACT\n\nUnclassified\nStandard\n\nForm 298 (Rev. 2-89)\n\n'
