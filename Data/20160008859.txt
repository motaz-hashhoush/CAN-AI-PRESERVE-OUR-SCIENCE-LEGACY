product life cycle\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nReduced part counts translate to reduced drawings, processes, configuration\nmanagement, increased reliability, shorter and simplified assembly and potentially a\nsmaller workforce and manufacturing footprint\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nReduced cost and schedule for parts translates to earlier risk reduction testing, reduced\nneed for early analysis, hardware available for early manufacturing and assembly trials.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nFlexible design options translate to lower mass, reduced interfaces and seals, increased\nperformance, and more efficient packaging.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nAdditive manufacturing properties, while still uncertain, are better than traditional cast parts\nallowing for reduced mass.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nProcuring hardware early while the design is immature allows for early iterations with vendors\nand the ability to gather test and assembly data to reduce long term cost and schedule impacts.\nEarly data feeds designs and analysis.\n\nNational Aeronautics and Space Administration\n\n12\n\nSummary\n\xe2\x80\xa2 AM has significant advantages over traditional manufacturing especially for complex parts\no
