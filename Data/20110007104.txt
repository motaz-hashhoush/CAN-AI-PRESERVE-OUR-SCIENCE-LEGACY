Hoberecht\nNational Aeronautics and Space Administration\nGlenn Research Center\nCleveland, Ohio 44135\n\nAbstract\nNASA\xe2\x80\x99s Exploration Technology Development Program funded the Energy Storage Project\nto develop battery and fuel cell technology to meet the expected energy storage needs of the\nConstellation Program for human exploration. Technology needs were determined by\narchitecture studies and risk assessments conducted by the Constellation Program, focused on\na mission for a long-duration lunar outpost. Critical energy storage needs were identified as\nbatteries for EVA suits, surface mobility systems, and a lander ascent stage; fuel cells for the\nlander and mobility systems; and a regenerative fuel cell for surface power. To address these\nneeds, the Energy Storage Project developed advanced lithium-ion battery technology, targeting\ncell-level safety and very high specific energy and energy density. Key accomplishments include\nthe development of silicon composite anodes, lithiated-mixed-metal-oxide cathodes, lowflammability electrolytes, and cell-incorporated safety devices that promise to substantially\nimprove battery performance while providing a high level of safety. The project also developed\n\xe2\x80\x9cnon-flow-through\xe2\x80\x9d proton-exchange-membrane fuel cell stacks. The primary advantage of this\ntechnology set is the reduction of ancillary parts in the balance-of-plant \xe2\x80\x93 fewer pumps,\nseparators and related components should result in fewer failure modes and hence a higher\nprobability of achieving very reliable operation, and reduced parasitic power losses enable\nsmaller reactant tanks and therefore systems with lower mass and volume. Key\naccomplishments include the fabrication and testing of several robust, small-scale non-flowthrough fuel cell stacks that have demonstrated proof-of-concept. This report summarizes the\nproject\xe2\x80\x99s goals, objectives, technical accomplishments, and risk assessments. A bibliography\nspanning the life of the project is also included.\n\nNASA/TM\xe2\x80\x942011-216963\n\n1\n\nOverview\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Project Goals and Objectives\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Summary of ABSTRACT\n\nNASA\xe2\x80\x99s Exploration Technology Development Program funded the Energy Storage Project to develop battery and fuel cell technology to\nmeet the expected energy storage needs of the Constellation Program for human exploration. Technology needs were determined by\narchitecture studies and risk assessments conducted by the Constellation Program, focused on a mission for a long-duration lunar outpost.\nCritical energy storage needs were identified as batteries for EVA suits, surface mobility systems, and a lander ascent stage; fuel cells for the\nlander and mobility systems; and a regenerative fuel cell for surface power. To address these needs, the Energy Storage Project developed\nadvanced lithium-ion battery technology, targeting cell-level safety and very high specific energy and energy density. Key accomplishments\ninclude the development of silicon composite anodes, lithiated-mixed-metal-oxide cathodes, low-flammability electrolytes, and cellincorporated safety devices that promise to substantially improve battery performance while providing a high level of safety. The project\nalso developed \xe2\x80\x9cnon-flow-through\xe2\x80\x9d proton-exchange-membrane fuel cell stacks. The primary advantage of this technology set is the\nreduction of ancillary parts in the balance-of-plant--fewer pumps, separators and related components should result in fewer failure modes\nand hence a higher probability of achieving very reliable operation, and reduced parasitic power losses enable smaller reactant tanks and\ntherefore systems with lower mass and volume. Key accomplishments include the fabrication and testing of several robust, small-scale nonflow-through fuel cell stacks that have demonstrated proof-of-concept. This report summarizes the project\xe2\x80\x99s goals, objectives, technical\naccomplishments, and risk assessments. A bibliography spanning the life of the project is also included.\n15.
