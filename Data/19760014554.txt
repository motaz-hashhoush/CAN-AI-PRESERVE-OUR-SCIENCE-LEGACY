To test the viability of interpretation of the imagery taken from the ERTS-1 satellite for the purpose of obtaining informa tion on areas occupied with coffee plantations and the introduction of\ncontour planting (as oposed to the standard square-planting technique).\nThis information would indicate variations in the area and tech\nniques of planting and therefore reflect production.\n2 - To verify which MSS sensor channel or channel combina\ntions would permit the best identification of coffee plantations.\n3 - To verify the season in which coffee plantations are more\neasily identified.\n\nSURVEYED AREA\nIn accordance with the proposal presented to NASA the researchwas based on interpretation and comparison of the following frames which\ninclude part of the proposed area (between the latitude 22900\' and\n23900\' S and longitude 51900\' and 52900\' W Gr.): frame numbers 1123 \t\n\n-\n\n12510 of November 23,1972;1213-12511 of February, 21,1973 and 1231-12512\nof March, 11,1973.\n\nREpR01)MIBILM OF THE\nORIGINAL PAGt IS POOR\n\nF\n\n2\nd\n\nMATERIAL USED IN THE RESEARCH\nThe MSS images used in the research correspond to bulk type.\n1 -
