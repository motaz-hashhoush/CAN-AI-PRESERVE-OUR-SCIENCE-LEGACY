Abstract\nIn Task D.6 of the Advanced Engine Study, three primary subtasks were accomplished:\n1) Design and Parametric Data, 2) Engine Requirement Variation Studies, and 3) Vehicle\nStudy/Engine Study Coordination.\nParametric data were generated for vacuum thrusts ranging from 7500 Ibf to 50000 Ibf, nozzle\nexpansion ratios from 600 to 1200, and engine mixture ratios from 5:1 to 7:1. the Failure Modes\nand Effects Analysis (FMEA) driven baseline design generated in Tasks D.4 and D.5 was used\nas a departure point for these parametric analyses. These data are intended to assist in vehicle\ndefinition and trade studies.\nIn the Engine Requirements Variation Studies, the individual effects of increasing the throttling\nratio from 10:1 to 20:1 and requiring the engine to operate at a maximum mixture ratio of 12:1\nwere determined. Off-design engine balances were generated at these extreme conditions and\nindividual component operating requirements analyzed in detail. Potential problems were\nidentified and possible solutions generated.\nIn the Vehicle Study/Engine Study coordination subtask, vehicle contractor support was provided\nas needed, addressing a variety of issues uncovered during vehicle trade studies. This support\nwas primarily provided during Technical Interchange Meetings (\'riM) In which Space Exploration\nInitiative (SEI) studies were addressed.\n\n18. Distribution Statement\n\n17. Abstract\nIn Task D.6 of the Advanced Engine Study, three primary subtasks were accomplished:\n1) Design and Parametric Data, 2) Engine Requirement Variation Studies, and 3) Vehicle\nStudy/Engine Study Coordination.\nParametric data were generated for vacuum thrusts ranging from 7500 Ibf to 50000 Ibf, nozzle\nexpansion ratios from 600 to 1200, and engine mixture ratios from 5:1 to 7:1. the Failure Modes\nand Effects Analysis (FMEA) driven baseline design generated in Tasks D.4 and D.5 was used\nas a departure point for these parametric analyses. These data are intended to assist in vehicle\ndefinition and trade studies.\nIn the Engine Requirements Variation Studies, the individual effects of increasing the throttling\nratio from 10:1 to 20:1 and requiring the engine to operate at a maximum mixture ratio of 12:1\nwere determined. Off-design engine balances were generated at these extreme conditions and\nindividual component operating requirements analyzed in detail. Potential problems were\nidentified and possible solutions generated.\nIn the Vehicle Study/Engine Study coordination subtask, vehicle contractor support was provided\nas needed, addressing a variety of Issues uncovered during vehicle trade studies. This support\nwas primarily provided during Technical Interchange Meetings (TIM) in which Space Exploration\nInitiative (SEI) studies were addressed.\n\n18. Distribution Statement\n\n17.
