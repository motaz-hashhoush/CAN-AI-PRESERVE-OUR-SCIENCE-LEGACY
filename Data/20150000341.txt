Another important advantage of using\nnear UV observations for aerosol characterization is the low surface albedo of all terrestrial\nsurfaces in this spectral region that reduces retrieval errors associated with land surface\nreflectance characterization. In spite of the 13 \xc3\x97 24 km2 coarse sensor footprint, the OMI near\nUV aerosol algorithm (OMAERUV) retrieves aerosol optical depth (AOD) and single-scattering\nalbedo under cloud-free conditions from radiance measurements at 354 and 388 nm. We present\nvalidation results of OMI AOD against space and time collocated Aerosol Robotic Network\nmeasured AOD values over multiple stations representing major aerosol episodes and regimes.\nOMAERUV\xe2\x80\x99s performance is also evaluated with respect to those of the Aqua-MODIS Deep\nBlue and Terra-MISR AOD algorithms over arid and semi-arid regions in Northern Africa. The\noutcome of the evaluation analysis indicates that in spite of the \xe2\x80\x9crow anomaly\xe2\x80\x9d problem,\naffecting the sensor since mid-2007, the long-term aerosol record shows remarkable sensor\nstability.\n\nSensor Name\n\nSept15 - 18, 2014, Aura Science Team Meeting\n\nTime Series\n\nMeaning of AOD Quality Assurance (QA) Flags\nQA Flag 0 (= most reliable retrievals)\nLevel 2.0 (= cloud screened and calibrated)\nQA Flag 3 (= very good confidence)\nQA Flags 0 and 1 (= successful aerosol mixtures)\nNo QA Flags\n\nAOD Collocation Method\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Within a radius of 40 km and \xc2\xb1 10 min window of the OMI overpass at 44 AERONET sites\nglobally distributed over lands.\n\nNon-absorbing,\n\nDust,\n\nSmoke\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 N : Total number of daily match-ups for 2005 - 2008.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Q = Percent of match-ups within \xc2\xb1 30 % or 0.1 AOD uncertainty limits.\n\xe2\x80\xa2 RMSE = SQRT[ SUM ( Y \xe2\x80\x93 LinearFitted Y )^2 / N ].
