For low R (less than approximately\n0.5), da/dN is influenced by\ncrack closure behavior. At higher R (> 0.5), a maximum stress-intensity\nfactor (Ka_) dependence is observed.\nFatigue crack growth behavior is\naffected by test temperature\nbetween 24 and 177 \xc2\xb0C. For moderate to\nhigh applied cyclic-stress-intensity\nfactors (AK), the slope of the log\nda/dN versus log AK curve is lower in 177 \xc2\xb0C laboratory air than 24 \xc2\xb0C\nlaboratory air. The difference in slope results in lower values of da/dN\nfor exposure to 177 \xc2\xb0C laboratory air compared to room temperature\nlaboratory air. The onset of this temperature\neffect is dependent upon\nthe applied R.\nThis temperature\neffect has not been observed\nin\nultrahigh vacuum.\nSpecimen orientation\nhas been shown to affect the\nslope of the log da/dN versus log AK curve in the Paris regime.\nThe\ndamaging effect of laboratory air is more pronounced for specimens with\nthe applied load oriented 45 \xc2\xb0 to the longitudinal\nand long transverse\ndirections\ncompared\nto fatigue\ncrack growth in the T-L or L-T\norientations.\n\nIntroduction\n\nTitanium\ncomponents,\ncompared\nexample\n\nalloy\n\nbecause\n\nsystems\nthey\n\nhave\n\nto conventional\nskin on leading\n\nedges,\n\nof hours;\n\nthis work\n\nis to characterize\n\nsheet,\n\ntwo more\n(Ti\n(air)\n\na\n\nwhich\n\nhigher\n\nthe\n\nspecific\n\nalloys.\n\nmay\n\nprecludes\n\nconsidered\n\nSome\n\noperate\n\nand\n\ncomponents\n\ngrowth\n\nwidely\n\ncompared\n\nstudied\n\nsheet\n\nmaterials,\n\nCharacterization\nto\n\nan\n\ninert\n\nof the\n\na [_ titanium\nfatigue\n\nenvironment\n\nof the environmental\n\ninteractions\n\ncrack\n(ultrahigh\nwhich\n\nalloy\n\non\n\nthan\n\n130\n\nsuch\n\nadvanced\n\nbehavior\n\nvacuum)\ninfluence\n\n21S)\n\nwill\n\nlong term\n\nobjective\n\nand another\n\nused\ndurability\n\nto\n\nof\n\ntitanium\n\nthis behavior\n\nin an active\nbe\n\nfor\n\n\xc2\xb0C for tens-ofThe\n\nand to compare\n\ngrowth\n\nstability\n\nairframes,\n\nalloys.\n\nof one\n\n(Timetal\n\naircraft\n\nthermal\n\nsupersonic\n\naluminum\n\nbehavior\n\nsupersonic\n\nimproved\n\ngreater\n\nof conventional\n\ncrack\n\nadvanced\n\nstrength\n\nat temperatures\n\nthe use\n\nfatigue\n\nfor\n\nTi - ABSTRACT\n\n(Maximum\n\n200 words)\n\nFatigue crack growth rates of Ti 6-2-2-2-2 as a function of stress ratio, temperature (24 or 177 \xc2\xb0C), tensile\norientation and environment (laboratory air or ultrahigh vacuum) are presented.\nFatigue crack growth rates of Ti\n6-2-2-2-2 are also compared with two more widely used titanium alloys (Timetal 21S and Ti 6A1-4V). The\nfatigue crack growth rate (da/dN) of Ti 6-2-2-2-2 in laboratory air is dependent upon stress ratio (R), particularly\nin the near-threshold\nand lower-Paris regimes. For low R (less than approximately\n0.5), da/dN is influenced by\ncrack closure behavior.\nAt higher R (> 0.5), a maximum stress-intensity\nfactor (Kmax) dependence is observed.\nFatigue crack growth behavior is affected by test temperature between 24 and 177 \xc2\xb0C. For moderate to high\napplied cyclic-stress-intensity\nfactors (delta-K), the slope of the log da/dN versus log delta-K curve is lower in\n177 \xc2\xb0C laboratory air than 24 \xc2\xb0C laboratory air. The difference in slope results in lower values of da/dN for\nexposure to 177 \xc2\xb0C laboratory air compared to room temperature laboratory air. The onset of this temperature\neffect is dependent upon the applied R. This temperature effect has not been observed in ultrahigh vacuum.\nSpecimen orientation has been shown to affect the slope of the log da/dN versus log delta-K curve in the Paris\nregime.\n14.
