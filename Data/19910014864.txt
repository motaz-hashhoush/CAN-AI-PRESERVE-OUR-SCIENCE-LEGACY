It was an\noperational decision.\nQ: ED LOCKE (Thermo Electron Technologies)\nTDWR used in the tests at Orlando?\n\n- Can you give the characteristics\n\nof the\n\nA: STEVE CAMPBELL\n(MIT Lincoln Laboratory) - I\'ll give you the characteristics\nfor the\nTDWR as I recall them and our test bed is very similar to these characteristics.\nThe wave\nlength is 5 cm; the antenna diameter is 27 feet; the PRF is adaptive. We have an adaptive\nscheme where we scan at a low PRF of about 350 Htz. That allows us to identify\nunambiguously\nthe very long range echoes. We then adaptively select a PRF which\nminimizes second trip folding into the first trip. In particular we try to minimize the folding\naround the immediate airport region. As a practical matter our\nPRF goes from something on the order of 700 Htz up to about 1200 Htz. The pulse\nenergy of the Raetheon TDWR is a quarter of a gigawatt and the pulse length is one\nmicrosecond.\nOur pulse length make be a little bit shorter for technical reasons.
