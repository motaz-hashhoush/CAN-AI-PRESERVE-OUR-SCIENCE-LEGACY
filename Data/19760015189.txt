INTRODUCTION\n\nThe purpose of this report is to evaluate the antenna switching\n\ncharacteristics of the S-Band Quad Antennas for a typical Shuttle\n\nmission.\n\nThis information is needed in order to assess the required\n\n\nupdate period(s) for the S-Band Quad Antenna switch from the\n\nassociated Shuttle Orbiter computer.\n\nBackground information is given in Section II in order to explain the\n\noperation of the S-Band Quad Antennas, the antenna switch and the\n\nassociated Shuttle Orbiter computer interfaces.\n\nThis is followed by\n\n\nSection III which contains a summary-of attitude and trajectory\n\nprofiles of Reference Mission 2 with 3 options.\n\nSection IV describes\n\n\nthe operation of the ground-based Fortran computer programs used to\n\nsimulate automatic switching and mission activity, and this is followed\n\nby Section V which gives the results of the computer runs which were\n\nmade at the Johnson Space Center.\n\nFinally, in Section VI the results\n\n\nof the computer runs are combined with the trajectory and attitude\n\nprofiles to evaluate antenna switching characteristics for Reference\n\nMission 2 with a recommendation for the antenna switch update period.\n\n\n1.2-DN-B0203-001\n\nPage 2\n\n\nII. A maneuver at the first apogee essentially circularizes the\n\norbit, and rendezvous phasing with the satellite is begun.\n\nThe\n\n\nrendezvous is completed approximately one day later.\n\nAfter rendezvous and dockinq with the satellite, refurbishment operation\n\ncommences at 26 hours ground elapse time (GET).\n\nRefurbishment operations\n\n\nterminate at 46 hours GET at which time servicing equipment is stowed.\n\nAt 49 hours GET the orbit is adjusted to establish a perigee of 230 n.\n\nmi. and then a circularization maneuver is performed at perigee 48\n\nminutes later.
