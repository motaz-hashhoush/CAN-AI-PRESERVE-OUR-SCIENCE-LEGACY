Many detailed system and subsystem requirements are also\ncontained in the FTR (Flight Test Requirements) document\nReference B. In order to assure that the OFT test program can\nadequately meet all of these requirements, and/or define a need\nfor change or addition, preliminary entry trajectories are being\ndeveloped. These trajectories are intended to be used for preliminary system and subsystem analysis purposes and to provide\nan overview of what will be experienced during the program.\nThis design note presents the pertinent parameters for the First\nOFT mission. A brief outline of the procedures employed as well\nas graphical and tabular displays of the output from the GEMASS\nsimulation are presented for the trajectory from Entry interface\nto TAEM interface.\n3.0 Discussion\nIn order to obtain a viable entry trajectory for a given mission,\nrealistic initial conditions are necessary for any simulations\nwhich may be conducted. Such conditions are not identified at\n\nthis time and must be developed based upon the overall requirements of the mission. The requirements for this mission are\ntabulated in Table I and were extracted directly from the MFTAD\n(Reference A). The constraints utilized or resulting from this\ntrajectory development are also presented for comparison purposes.\nIn developing the initial conditions at entry interface it was\nfirst necessary to obtain a realistic orbit. For this purpose,\nreference was made to NASA General Working Paper No. 10,009\n(Reference C). The 120 nautical mile orbit was selected and the\norbital insertion conditions were extracted for use as initial\nconditions for a simulation of the orbital path in the GEMASS\nprogram as run in an orbital mode. The output of this program\nwas then used for establishing the orbital ground path (Figure\n1) which agreed almost precisely with the reference. This\norbital path, now . available to necessary accuracy, was used\nfor defining the cross range distance and providing the position (latitude and longitude) and velocity vector direction\nnecessary for any point along the path from which an entry\nsimulation was desired. The insertion conditions utilized for\nthis development are presented in Table II.\nIn order to define the velocity vector magnitude and flight\npath angle at entry interface an orbital retro program was\nutilized employing several different values of impulse each of\nwhich yielded different values of velocity, flight path angle,\nand earth central angle traversed to the 401; :000 foot interface\naltitude.\nFrom these data, several sets of initial conditions were defined\nas possible conditions for entry simulations. The initial conditions used were then selected based upon the output from the 3\nDOF (Degree-of-Freedom) GEMASS which yielded the results necessary to best meet the MFTAD and FTR re quirements. Based y pon the\nentry conditions selected. the orbit - retrof i re point was establisl\nbased upon the earth central angle traversed. These deorbit\n\nconditions are also presented in Table II.
