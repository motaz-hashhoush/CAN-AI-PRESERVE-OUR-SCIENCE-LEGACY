Aiken,\n\nG.F.\n\nMoore,\n\nC.C.\n\nTrees,\n\nS.B.\n\nHooker,\n\nand D.K.\n\nClark\n\nABSTRACT\nThe Sea-viewing\nWide Field-of-view\nSensor (SeaWiFS)\nmission will provide operational\nocean color that will be\nsuperior\nto the previous\nCoastal\nZone Color Sensor (CZCS) proof-of-concept\nmission.\nAn algorithm\nis needed\nthat exploits the full functionality\nof SeaWiFS whilst remaining\ncompatible\nin concept with algorithms\nused for\nthe CZCS. This document\ndescribes\nthe theoretical\nrationale\nof radiance\nband-ratio\nmethods\nfor determining\nchlorophyll\na and other important\nbiogeochemical\nparameters,\nand their implementation\nfor the SeaWIFS\nmission. Pigment\ninterrelationships\nare examined\nto explain the success of the CZCS algorithms.\nIn the context\nwhere chlorophyll\na absorbs only weakly at 520 nm, the success of the 520 nm to 550 nm CZCS band ratio needs to\nbe explained.\nThis is explained\nby showing that in pigment data from a range of oceanic provinces chlorophyll\na\n(absorbing\nat less than 490 nm), carotenoids\n(absorbing\nat greater than 460 nm), and total pigment\nare highly\ncorrelated.\nCorrelations\nwithin pigment groups particularly\nphotoprotectant\nand photosynthetic\ncarotenoids\nare\nless robust.\nThe sources of variability\nin optical data are examined\nusing the NIMBUS Experiment\nTeam (NET)\nbio-optical\ndata set and bio-optical\nmodel.\nIn both the model and NET data, the majority\nof the variance\nin\nthe optical data is attributed\nto variability\nin pigment (chlorophyll\na), and total particulates,\nwith less than 5%\nof the variability\nresulting\nfrom pigment\nassemblage.\nThe relationships\nbetween\nband ratios and chlorophyll\nis examined\nanalytically,\nand a new formulation\nbased on a dual hyperbolic\nmodel is suggested\nwhich gives a\nbetter calibration\ncurve than the conventional\nlog-log linear regression\nfit. The new calibration\ncurve shows the\n490:555 ratio is the best single-band\nratio and is the recommended\nCZCS-type\npigment\nalgorithm.\nUsing both\nthe model and NET data, a number of multiband\nalgorithms\nare developed;\nthe best of which is an algorithm\nbased on the 443:555 and 490:555 ratios.\nFrom model data, the form of potential\nalgorithms\nfor other products,\nsuch as total particulates\nand dissolved organic matter\n(DOM),\nare suggested.\n\n1.\n\nINTRODUCTION\n\nAs a second-generation\nocean color instrument,\nthe Seaviewing Wide Field-of-view\nSensor (SeaWiFS)\noffers a variety of design improvements\nover its predecessor\nthe Coastal\nZone Color Scanner\n(CZCS).\nThe design of the SeaWiFS\ninstrument\nwas driven by science requirements\nas defined\nby the SeaWiFS\nPrelaunch\nScience Working\nGroup (SPSWG). The SPSWG\nwas an ad hoc committee\nselected\nby the National\nAeronautics\nand Space Administration\n(NASA) Headquarters\nfor the purpose of providing\nNASA\nwith guidance\nin the formulation\nof mission objectives,\nspecifications,\nand goals. derived products:\nSeaWiFS baseline,\nCZCS-type,\nand potential\nSeaWiFS\nproducts.\nA differentiation\nis made between\nCZCS-type\npigment and SeaWiFS\nbaseline chlorophyll-like\npigment concentrations.\nThe SeaWiFS\nsemianalytical\nalgorithm\nfor\nchlorophyll\na will be developed\nusing analytical\nand semianalytical\nmodels.\nChlorophyll\na is the parameter\nthat has\nbeen chosen as it is regarded\nuniversally\nas the most appropriate\nmeasure\nof viable phytoplankton\nbiomass (i.e.,\nphytoplankton\nwhich are growing\nactively and capable of\ngrowth).\nThe CZCS-type\npigment\nproduct\nis supposed\nto provide some form of continuity\nwith the totM pigment\nprod-\n\nuct derived\nfrom CZCS imagery,\ntermed\nthe CZCS Pigment Algorithm\nproduct.\nThere is no predetermined\nconsensus for the rationale\nor definition\nof this product\n(chlorophyll a, photosynthetic\npigments,\nor total pigments).\nA\nchoice must be made at the outset,\nso a methodological\napproach\ncan be determined\nand described.\nThe proposed\napproach\nshould be essentially\nempirical,\nand use band ratios in common with the original CZCS algorithms.\nThere\nis a possible the standard\nNASA CZCS\ntwo-band\nalgorithm,\nused for the global processing,\ncould\nbe used unaltered.\nIt may be that an algorithm\nusing\nall three bands at 443, 520, and photoprotectant\npigments\nwhich co-exist, co-vary, and\nabsorb at the same wavelengths\nas chlorophyll\na, and which\noccur in significant\nconcentrations.\nIndividually,\nthey may\naccount for approximately\n5-50% of the total pigment concentration\nand, in combination\nwith chlorophyll\na, may\n\ncome as a result of the development\nof analyses\nusing\nthe high performance\nliquid chromatography\n(HPLC) technique (Mantoura\nand Llewellyn 1983 and Trees et al. 1985),\nwhich is the recommended\nmethodology\nin the Ocean Optics Protocols for Sea WiFS Validation\n(Mueller and Austin\n1995).\nAlong with the insight on pigments\nfor some has come\nconfusion\nfor others, with earlier reports\nthat concentrations of pigments\ndetermined\nby HPLC and fluorescence\n(Trees et al.
