reliability\ncan be defined for ceramics. The first is\na statistical\nreliability,\nas illustrated\nin figure 4. Ceramics typically\ndisplay a broad distribution\nof strengths.\nIn the inspection approach to\nreliability,\nwe would separate unacceptable parts by NDEand proof testing and\nwould reject them. A more efficient\nand cost effective approach lies in\nimproved processing that increases strength and yields no defective parts.\nWedefine the second form of reliability\nas functional reliability\nbecause\nit relates to how well a componentperforms its function during system assembly\nand service. Thus, factors such as fracture toughness, impact resistance, and\nfailure mode(graceful versus catastrophic) need to be considered. Specific\napproaches to improved functional reliability\ninclude the addition of\nparticulate and whisker phases which can improve fracture toughness, and the\naddition of continuous fibers which can both improve toughness and provide a\nnoncatastrophic failure mechanism.
