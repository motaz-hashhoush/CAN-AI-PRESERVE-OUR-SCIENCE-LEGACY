As part of the Terminal Configured Vehicle program (TCV),\nsponsored jointly by NASA and FAA, this study was undertaken to: (1) investigate the\ncompatability of existing Autoland guidance laws utilizing a Microwave Landing System\n(MLS) in place of the conventional ILS navigation aids during landing approach; and (2)\nto extend the concept of automated flight to rollout arid turnoff for reduced runway\noccupancy during normal and adverse (CAT III) landing conditions. In order to achieve\nacceptable tracking performance in dry and adverse weather conditions, a buried magnetic runway cable and an aircraft mounted three coil magnetic pickup were investigated.\nFor the purpose of the study, a dynamical model of the B737 was generated in\na computer program (ALERT) to simulate the aircraft forces and moments during landing approach, rollout and turnoff. A simulation of the existing Autoland guidance\n\nix\n\nequations for the B737 was supplied by the Langley Research Center* Optimal Kalman\nfiltering, utilizing a square root formulation for onboard implementation, and a nonoptimal fixed gain complementary filter were studied. A rollout and turnoff guidance\nlaw was generated. Both Kalman type filtering and fixed gain complementary filtering\nare shown to be useable with little change to conventional guidance equations presently\nin use in commercial aircraft.\nThis report includes a study of the requirements for the Kalman filter and the\nrollout a n d turnoff guidance laws.\n\n...\n\n:;\n\nI; :i\n\nAIRCRAFT DYNAMICS \xe2\x80\xa2 = " "\n\n.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2\'.\xe2\x80\xa2-..\n\n.-...\n\n:\xe2\x80\xa2\xe2\x80\xa2 .\n\n- .\xe2\x80\xa2\n\n...-.-.-,.\n\n""\' > \' . - \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 \'\n:\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 . \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 \' . \xe2\x80\xa2 . : ; < - : \xe2\x80\xa2 / . \' K\' : : . : \xe2\x80\xa2 "\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 .- <\n\n= i \' i "\xe2\x80\xa2\n\n.\n\n:;\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 - \xe2\x80\xa2 - . - \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2\n\xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 v \xe2\x80\xa2;\xe2\x80\xa2\'\n\n:\n\n, \';.\' \xe2\x80\xa2 \' \'\n\n:\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 . \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 \' \xe2\x80\xa2 . \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 \xe2\x80\xa2 : \' ("\'\n\n.
