Each author included is listed by organizational\naffiliation, address, and principal field of expertise. Multiple listings appear\nwhere they seem appropriate.\nThe criteria for the selection of names for the Register are recent contributions\nto the literature, participation in or support of relevant research programs,\nand referral by peers.\nThe purpose of this publication is to present, in easy reference form, sources\nfor dependable information regarding failure modes and mechanisms of aerospace\nstructures or for other structure subjected to comparable environments. The\nexperts, who are points of contact, could be queried by researchers in similar\nfields of research and development work for unpublished information and more\nof the details which havc not appeared 4n r%1lishaA rpnnrtq journ1l qrtirlps,\nIn addition, further inquiries could be made regarding\nor society presentations.\nactive ongoing projects where the published results may not appear for several\nmonths. No attempt was made to ascertain that persons listed are actually\navailable for extensive consultation. This is left to negotiation between the\nparties involved.\nThe organizational affiliation reflects the latest reference document which\nwas reviewed and is not necessarily current.\nFinal selection for inclusion was based on the independent judgments of an\nauthor\'s peers as well as the number of his appropriate documents uncovered by\na staff of technical reviewers. This is recognized as being an incomplete\nlisting. Nevertheless, we hope that it will be useful as a guide to those who\nseek related information.\n\nvi\n\n4.\n\nHydrogen embrittlement of superalloys. This subject is of interest\nregarding turbine buckets, which are exposed to high temperatures. It\nwill be of increasing importance if additional interest develops in\nusing hydrogen as the fuel in gas turbine engines.\n\n5.\n\nProtective coatings. Airbreathing engines operating in contaminated\nenvironments are in need of protection against attack by the contaminants.\nInformation on the various candidate coatings and the efforts of combustion\nproducts of contaminants in jet fuels on engine components is of prime\ninterest. For example, the sulfur ordinarily contained in JP fuels reacts\nwith salt present in shipboard and offshore environments and the resulting\ncompounds attack turbine buckets severely.\n\n6.\n\nComposite materials data on low cycle and thermal fatigue. The aim was\nto search for data related to composite structural materials.\n\nThe period of search for published information was June 1962 to April 1974\nalthough earlier publications of classic value were considered. The degree\nof thoroughness of the search for published experts has been controlled by\nthree factors:\n(1) the responsiveness of candidate experts; (2) contractual\nlimitations in the form of budget and time; and (3).the priority of the compilation of the Register with respect to other contract tasks.\nUnder Contract NAS 3-16681 four reports were published: NASA CR-121199,\nThesaurus of Terms for Information on Mechanics of Structural Failure;\nNASA CR-121201, Register of Specialized Sources for Information on the Mechanics\nof Structural Failure; NASA CR-121200, Register of Experts for Information on\nthe Mechanics of Structural Failure; and NASA CR-121202, Bibliography of\nInformation on the Mechanics of Structural Failure. Under the current contract,\nNAS 3-17640, the last two of these reports are being updated, revised, and\nexpanded. Much of their revision is directly related to two new tasks under\nContract NAS 3-17640. These are the preparation of two technology survey\nreports, one each in Problem Area No. 1 and No. 2. Because of the particular\nemphasis in study on those two problem areas the Register is somewhat unbalanced\nin its listings.\nConsiderable effort has been made to assure that the persons listed are in\nfact still working in the fields of interest. Even so it is not possible to\nguarantee that the expert is available for consultation or that he is at the\naddress shown. Retirees who are still active professionally are listed at\ntheir last affiliation unless they indicated a preferred alternate address.\nOther experts are listed at the organizational affiliation shown in their\nlatest referenced publication unless direct inquiry resulted in a more recent\naddress. In no case have the authors attempted to ascertain that the persons\nlisted are actually available for extensive negotiation. This is left to\nnegotiation between them and other interested parties.\n\nviii\n\nINTRODUCTION\n\nA prime asset in any research program is the ready availability of resources\nincluding people. The NASA Lewis Research Centers Aerospace Safety Research\nand Data Institute (ASRDI) investigation of the mechanics of structural\nfailure and structural materials limitations has included the task of identifying\nthe most knowledgeable people doing work in the areas under study. To\ndevelop a useful list, i.e., one that potentially was comprised of experts\navailable for consultation, an effort has been made to personally contact\neach individual listed in this Register.\nSince June 1972, the Orlando Division of Martin Marietta Aerospace has been\nASRDI\'s agent for the compilation and publication of a Register of Experts\nfor Information on the Mechanics of Structural Failure. An initial issue,\nNASA CR-121200, was published in June 1973 under NASA Contract NAS 3-16681.\nThe authors searched various directories but found none of significant help.\nA November 1966 Battelle publication, "Roster of the U.S. Government Metalas one of the few pcrtinet documen--ts\nworking Processes nnd E,iinpmPnt Program,"\nof value.\nIn the end information had to be obtained through letters of inquiry\nand personal visits. Throughout the investigation the criteria for being\nincluded in the Register has been referral by peers and current publication\nin areas of interest in the mechanics of structural failure.\nThe six problem areas of interest in the mechanics of structural failure are:\n1.\n\nLife prediction of materials exposed to monotonic and cyclic loadings.\nInitially attention was primarily focused only on low cycle fatigue and\nthermal fatigue at elevated temperatures (equivalent to those in the hot\nend of a gas turbine engine) and on high cycle fatigue data for materials\nused in components such as engine bearings.\nSubsequently the area of\nsurvey was expanded to include consideration of solar cell applications,\nthe effect of cryogenic temperature and vacuum environments, and radiation\neffects.\n\n2.\n\nFracture toughness data on various structural materials. Available data\nare categorized with respect to test methods KIc vs Kc and other peculiar\nparameters considered by the investigators.\nIn particular, data derived\nfrom ASTM standard tests are identified.\n\n3.\n\nFracture mechanics analyses - capabilities and limitations. A significant\nnumber of publications deal with linear elastic fracture mechanics and\nassume plane strain. Attempts were made to identify any work that was\ndone, taking into account elastic-plastic theories.\n\nvii\n\nPROBLEM AREA 1\n\nLife prediction of materials exposed to monotonic and\ncyclic loading - Experts in the life prediction of\nmaterials subject to creep, low-cycle fatigue and thermal fatigue are presented. Particular areas of emphasis\ninclude the thermal/mechanical environment of the hot\nend of the gas turbine engine and the initiation and\npropagation of fatigue cracks in smooth and precracked\nspecimens. Other areas included are the interactions\nbetween creep, fatigue and the environment at elevated\ntemperatures; thermal and mechanical fatigue effects\non solar cells in the cryogenic vacuum environment;\nradiation effects on high temperature mechanical properties; and high cycle fatigue technology as applicable\nto gas turbine engine bearings.\n\n\'U!\n\nExpert\n\nExpertise\n\nOrganization\nABELKIS, Paul R.\nMcDonnell Douglas Company\nLong Beach, California\n\nACHTER, M.
