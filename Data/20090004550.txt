Gaier\nNASA Glenn Research Center\nCleveland OH 44135\nLargely due to rock and soil samples returned during the Apollo program, much has been learned\nabout the composition and properties of lunar regolith. Although, for the most part, the mineral\ncomposition resembles terrestrial minerals, the characteristics of the lunar environment have led\nto very different weathering processes. These result in substantial differences in the particle\nshapes, particle size distributions, and surface chemistry. These differences lead to non-intuitive\nadhesion, abrasion, and possible health properties that will pose challenges to future lunar\nmissions. An overview of lunar dust composition and properties will be given with a particular\nemphasis on possible health effects.\n\nNational Aeronautics and Space Administration\n\nLunar Dust 101\n\nJames R.
