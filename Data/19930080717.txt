The length-width ratio of all bodies\nis 6:1; all show the same circular cross sections as the body of rotation and consequently, all have the same longitudinal volume distribution\n(fig. 1). They differ solely by the form of the axis of gravity of\ntheir cross sections, that is, the line connecting the centers of the\nCircles, thus their center line.\nIn case of the body of rotation A this center line is the axis\ngoing through the main -bulkhead plane.\n\nFor the four round bodies derived from it the center line is for\nbody\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nB - cambered in front\n\nC - cambered toward the rear\nD - cambered in the same sense on both sides\nE - cambered in the opposite sense on both sides\nThe extent of these two-dimensional cambers is determined by the\ncircular cross sections being shifted in each case until they touch a\nplane parallel to the center axis and tangent to the main bulkhead.\n\n* \'Momente von gekrlimmten Rundkorpern.
