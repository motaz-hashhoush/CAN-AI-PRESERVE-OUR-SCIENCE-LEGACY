selected 40 scientific investigations \xe2\x80\x94\nthe U.S. and seven from four foreign nations \xe2\x80\x94\n\n33 from\n\nto be studied\n\nand developed for a series of Spacelab/Shuttle flights astronomy,\nupper atmospheric physics, space plasma physics, solar physics\nand high energy astrophysics.\n\nForty principal investigators have been selected from 16\nuniversities, four private organizations or companies and seven\ndifferent government agencies.\n\nOver 250 coinvestigators are\n\nassociated with the selected investigations.\n\nSpacelab will be carried to and from orbit by the Space\nShuttle and remain attached to the Orbiter throughout the\nflight where it will serve as a platform for investigations\nin near-Earth orbit for a period of about one week.\n\nAlmost 200 responses were received by NASA from the world\nscientific community in reply to an Announcement of Opportunity\nfor Spacelab science investigations sent out by the space\nagency last June.\n\nWith the advent of the Space Shuttle and the availability\nof standard space qualified hardware which can be placed in\norbit, repaired, retrieved or replaced, NASA plans to exploit\nthis capability and reduce the cost of such payloads while\nmaking space flight more accessible to a wider range of users.\n-more-\n\n-3-\n\nIt is intended that these investigations will be covered\nby new procedures which no longer require the investigator to\nadhere to the strict performance characteristics that had to\nbe demonstrated prior to flight in former years.\n\nNow investi-\n\ngations must comply only with flight safety requirements and\ngood engineering and management practices as provided in NASA\npolicy guidelines.\n\nInvestigations selected for development\n\nare expected to be assigned to a flight by NASA when it is\ndetermined that the investigators can meet their planned\nobjective and a specific flight delivery date.\n\n# # # # #\n\nA list of investigations is
