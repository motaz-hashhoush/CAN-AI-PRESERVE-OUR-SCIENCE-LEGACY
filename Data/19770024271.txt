This profiLe updates the preliminary\n\nOFT-1 deorbit-through-landing reference fLight profiLe (ref. 1). DetaiLed de\xc2\xad\n\nscriptions and the rationaLe for the shaping of each phase are incLuded.\n\n\nSince reference 1 was published, the projected Orbiter weight during entry\n\nhas increased from 181 450 pounds to 183 839.8 pounds, and the circular or\xc2\xad\n\nbital altitude prior to deorbit has increased from approximately 120 nautical\n\n\nmiles to approximately 150 nautical miles. The deorbit-through-Landing refer\xc2\xad\n\nence \n\nflight profile described in this document has been reshaped to accommodate\n\n\nthese changes and to provide compatibility with manual flight in the approach\n\n\nand landing phase. In addition, the angle-of-attack profile during the (transi\xc2\xad\n\ntion phase of entry has been modified slightly.\n\n\nThe Orbiter surface temperatures during entry for this profiLe have been\n\n\nmaintained at approximately the same Orbiter surface temperatures of the pre\xc2\xad\n\nvious profile (ref. 1). Consequently, the heat Load during entry for this.\n\n\nprofiLe has increased slightly from that of the previous profile because of the\n\n\nenergy that must be dissipated during entry as a result of increased Orbiter\n\n\nweight and orbital altitude.\n\n\nProviding compatibility with manual flight for the approach and landing\n\n\nphase results in changes to the outer and inner glideslopes from 24 degrees and\n\n\n3 degrees to 22 degrees and 1.5 degrees, respectively. The modified angle-of\xc2\xad\n\nattack profile during the transition phase of entry provides improved trajectory\n\n\'control at initiation of the ramp from the 40-degree angle of attack required\n\n\nfor the high heating region of entry to the lower angle of attack required at\n\n\nthe entry/terminal area energy management (TAEM) interface and provides more\n\n\nmargin for dispersions at the entry/TAEM interface.\n\n\nThe TAEM flight profile has been reshaped to accommodate the 22-degree\n\n\nglideslope at the TAEM/approach and landing interface; to accommodate the lower\n\n\ndynamic pressure and higher angle of attack at entry/TAEM interface caused bY\n\n\n\n1-1\n\n\n\nthe modified angLe-of-attack profile during entry, and to provide better flight\n\n\ncontrol conditions in the TAEM phase.\n\n\nThe anaLysis performed in shaping the OFT-1 deorbit-through-Landing profile\n\n\nhas resuLted in requirements to modify the entry and TAEM guidance software. A\n\n\nmodification to the entry guidance software is required to provide better con\xc2\xad\n\ntrol of phugoid damping for the higher drag Levels necessary because of in\xc2\xad\n\ncreased orbiter weight and increased orbiter aLtitudes. A TAEM guidance soft\xc2\xad\n\nware modification is necessary to provide a better match between the reference\n\n\nprofiLe defined by the entry guidance logic and the reference profiLe defined\n\n\nby the TAEM guidance Logic at the entry/TAEM interface.\n\n\nThe modification to the entry guidance software has been approved by the\n\n\nOrbiter Avionics Software ControL Board (OASCB). A change request for the modi\xc2\xad\n\nfications to the TAEM guidance has been submitted to the OASCB.\n\n\nOFT-i will be Launched from Kennedy Space Center (KSC) into an approximate\n\n\n150-nautical miLe altitude circuLar orbit with a 38-degree incLination. The\n\n\ndeorbit maneuver is initiated at 29 hours 40 minutes 03 seconds ground eLapsed\n\n\ntime (GET) during the 20th orbit, with subsequent Landing on runway 17 left on\n\n\nRogers Lake bed at Edwards Air Force Base (EAFB) at 10 hours 58 minutes 50 sec\xc2\xad\n\nonds Local time. The 20th orbit was selected for deorbit because this deor\xc2\xad\n\nbit opportunity provides communications and tracking by the Guam station between\n\n\ndeorbit and entry interface and an opportunity for backup deorbit on the follow\xc2\xad\n\ning orbit. FoLLowing this backup deorbit opportunity, the next deorbit pos\xc2\xad\n\nsibiLity is about 17.7 hours Later during the 33rd orbit. The entry ground\n\n\ntracks are generally north of, and clear of, the denseLy populated Los Angeles\n\n\narea for deorbit during orbits 17 through 21.\n\n\nThe deorbit targeting is biased to provide the capability to achieve the\n\n\ndesired entry interface state vector with two orbiter maneuvering system (OMS)\n\n\nengines as pLanned for the normaL deorbit maneuver or with one OMS engine in\n\n\ncontingency situations. The minimum deorbit AV for this biased targeting is\n\n\n364 fps. Flight\n\nsoftware Logic has been impLemented to provide yaw steering during ascent\n\n\nif an AOA to EAFB is required. This reduces the crossrange-at-entry interface\n\n\nto within the 550 nautical mile crossrange requirement for OFT-1. The approach\n\n\nand Landing profiLe has been shaped to provide a 22-degree outer glidesLope\n\n\nand a 1.5-degree inner glideslope that is compatible with manual fLight.\n\n\nSeveral assumptions and simplifications in systems modeling were made\n\n\nin developing flight corridors and flight profiLes for the OFT-1 deorbit\xc2\xad\n\nthrough-landing profile. ALso, constraints and guideLines for different\n\n\nOrbiter systems and for operational considerations resulted in conflicting\n\n\nrequirements in the OFT-1 flight profile development. Therefore, detailed\n\n\nOrbiter systems and operations evaluation of the profile is required to determine\n\n\nthe adequacy of the profiLe.\n\n\nThe OFT-1 deorbit-through-landing profile will be refined and updated\n\n\nas issues are resoLved.\n\n\n\n7-I\n\n\n\n8.0\n\nREFERENCES\n\n\n\n1. \tHiott, James M.: OFT-1 Preliminary Reference FLight Profile for Deorbit\n\n\nThrough Landing. The incLination has been selected to op\xc2\xad\n\ntimize insertion and onorbit Spaceflight Tracking and Data Network (STDN)\n\n\ncoverage within limits resulting from entry crossrange and aerodynamic heating\n\n\nconsiderations.\n\n\nFor al- OFT flights, AOA landings will be on runway 17L on Rogers Lake bed at\n\n\nEAFB, and landings for RTLS will- be on runway 15 at KSC.. Because of the high\n\n\nprobability of landing on either runway 15 or 33 for RTLS, OFT performance\n\n\nassessment will be based on the capabiLity to achieve either runway-for RTLS.\n\n\nThe time from the end of the last sleep period prior to deorbit to touchdown\n\n\nwill not exceed 12 hours.\n\n\nNominaL landing, landing following AOA, and RTLS aborts will be no earlier than\n\n\n0.5 hour after sunrise nor Later than 0.5 hour prior to sunset. It is desirable\n\n\nthat landings at EAFB occur prior to 1000 hours local time.\n\n\nThe onorbit attitude time line and predeorbit thermal conditioning will minimize\n\n\nTPS backface temperature at entry interface within acceptabLe limits.\n\n\nAOA capability and contingency deorbit capability on at least the first four\n\n\nrevolutions following lift-off will be provided with landing at EAFB.\n\n\nAt all points on the ascent profile, the Orbiter will have RTLS or AOA capabili\xc2\xad\n\nty, and the RTLS/AOA abort overlap will be maximized consistent with system\n\n\nmargins and AOA crossrange limitations.\n\n\n\n2.0\n\nDEORBIT\n\n\n\nPropulsive maneuvers, venting, and attitude time lines shall be designed to\n\n\nenhance state vector determination during atmospheric descent for nominal end\xc2\xad\n\nof-mission flight termination and contingencies.\n\n\nThe IMU dlinement shall be designed to minimize the IMU misalinement at the\n\n\nentry interface. The maximum platform misalinement at -entry interface- for a\n\n\nstar tracker alinement shall be TBD.\n\n\nAn IMU aLinement will be made as close as possible to deorbit burn but no longer\n\n\nthan 1.5 hours before the deorbit burn.\n\n\n\nA-3\n\n\n\nThe auxiliary power units (APU\'s) wilL be assumed to be turned on one orbit\n\n\nbefore deorbit TIG and will remain on for approximately 8.5 minutes for APU\n\n\nand flight control system checkout. They will then be shut down until 5 minutes\n\n\nbefore deorbit ignition and will remain on throughout Landing and rollout.\n \n\nThe deorbit attitude wilL be achieved at a minimum of 5 minutes prior to deorbit\n\n\nburn initiation.\n\n\nDeorbit burns will be targeted out of pLane, if necessary, to provide acceptable\n\n\ncenter-of-gravity conditions at entry interface.\n\n\nThe deorbit maneuver will be performed with both OMS engines, but an acceptable\n\n\ndeorbit must be achieved even if one of these engines does not operate success\xc2\xad\n\nfully.\n\n\nPropellant-critical contingency deorbit will be based on a shallower-than\xc2\xad\n\nnominal targeting criteria where this targeting provides the best compromise\n\n\nbetween deorbit capability, RCS propellant availability for attitude control\n\n\nduring atmospheric descent, and entry thermal environment.\n\n\nALL nominal deorbit opportunities will be planned so that a backup opportunity\n\nexists on the next revolution with a crossrange of less than 550 nautical miles.\n\n\nA minimum free-fall time of 15 minutes between the termination of the deorbit\n\n\nmaneuver with one OMS failure and entry interface is required for entry prepa\xc2\xad\n\nration.,\n\n\nAPU hydraulic thermal conditioning will be performed between deorbit and entry\n\n\ninterface.\n\n\nIn addition to satisfying the entry velocity, flightpath angle, and range re\xc2\xad\n\nquirements, the deorbit maneuver will reduce the OMS propellant remainingto\n\n\nachieve an acceptable Orbiter entry weight and center of gravity.\n\n\n3.0\n\nENTRY\n\n\n\nThe entry profile will be shaped to minimize TPS bondline temperatures while\n\n\nmaintaining surface temperatures within limits and providing acceptable systems\n\n\nand trajectory margins to compensate for dispersions.\n\n\nThe Orbiter entry weight will be minimized by reducing residual consumables,\n\n\nsuch as OMS and forward RCS propellant, within safe limits. Nominal RCS pro\xc2\xad\n\npellant allowance for attitude control for entry through landing will be\n\n\n2250 pounds in the aft RCS propellant tanks. However, maximum aft RCS propel\xc2\xad\n\nlant, consistent with mission objectives and center-of-gravity considerations,\n\n\nwill be maintained for entry-through-landing attitude controL.\n\n\nThe entry-through-landing profile will conform to structural load limits corre\xc2\xad\n\nsponding to a -0.8 to 2g normal Load factor, to control surface hinge moment\n\n\naerodynamics load limits, and to actuator rate limits.\n\n\n\nA-4\n\n\n\nThe maximum normal load factor with Orbiter weight equal to or less than 189 000\n\n\npounds shall be limited to 2g\'s nominally and 2.5g\'s for contingencies. Planned\n\n\nnominaL entry crossrange wiLL be < 550 n.mi. with an angle-of-attack profile of\n\n\n40 degrees.\n \n\nOptimization of the entry profile will include consideration of sonic boom\n \n\nground-level overpressures.\n\n\nNominal and abort targeting will be designed so that postblackout target changes\n \n\nare not nominally required. This does not preclude targeting to provide profile\n\n\nshaping to maximize the capability to redesignate after exit from voice\n\n\ncommunications blackout.\n\n\nThe nominal Orbiter center of gravity will be 66.25 percent longitudinally at\n\n\nentry with no lateral center-of-gravity displacement. The nominal vertical\n\n\ncenter of gravity will be 375 + 3 inches.\n\n\nThe TAEM guidance target dynamic pressure will be based on the concept of flying\n\n\ndirectly to the HAC without employing a procedural turn in tailwind conditions.\n\n\nAdditionally, this dynamic pressure will avoid undershoot conditions in the\n\n\npresence of severe headwinds. The energy control will provide conditions suit\xc2\xad\n\nable for the initiation of autoland on the final approach.\n\n\nThe terminal area profile will be compatible with either a manual or an automat\xc2\xad\n\nic mode of operation.\n\n\nThe maximum dynamic pressure during TAEM will be limited to 342 psf. The choice\n\n\nof minimum dynnamic pressure assures that terminal area maneuvers are limited\n\n\nto the front side of the lift/drag curve. Therefore, the minimum dynamic\n\n\npressure is a function of Orbiter weight and varies from 138 psf to 161 psf\n\n\nas the orbiter weight varies from 188K to 218K Lbs.\n\n\nTerminal area maneuvers wilL not require operation on the backside of the\n\n\nlift/drag curve.\n\n\nNominal touchdown speed and altitude rate will provide adequate pitch margins\n\n\nfor structural clearance, maximum tire speed margins, and landing gear struc\xc2\xad\n\ntural load margins.\n\n\nThe TAEM guidance will not command a bank angle greater than 30 degrees for Mach\n\n\nnumbers above 0.9 nor 60 degrees below Mach 0.9.\n\n\nThe maximum descent rate at landing shall be limited to 7 fps for an Orbiter\nweight < 187 800 pounds.\nThe design landing weight of 188 000 pounds will not be exceeded for nominal\n\n\nLandings.
