When compared to\nchemical propulsion,\nthe Hall thruster system can add additional spacecraft per launch using planned payload power\nlevels. One satellite can be added to the assumed four satellite baseline chemical launch without additional mission times.\nTwo or three satellites may be added by providing part of the orbit insertion with the Hall system. In these cases orbit\ninsertion times wcrc found to be 35 and 62 days. Depending on the electric propulsion scenario, the resulting launch\nvehicle savings is nearly two, three or four Delta 7920 launch vehicles out of the chemical baseline scenario\'s eight Delta\n7920 launch vehicles.\n\n14.\n\nSUBJECT\n\nElectric\n\nTERMS\n\npropulsion;\n\n15.\n\nLEO satellite;\n\n16.\n\nHall thruster\n\nNUMBER\n\nOF\n\nPRICE\n\n20.\n\nLIMITATION\n\nPAGES\n\nCODE\n\nAO,_\n17.\n\nSECURITY\nOF\n\nCLASSIFICATION\n\nREPORT\n\nUnclassified\n\n18.\n\nSECURITY\nOF\n\nTHIS\n\nCLASSIFICATION\nPAGE\n\nUnclassified\n\n19.\n\nSECURITY\nOF\n\nCLASSIFICATION\n\nOF\n\nABSTRACT\n\nABSTRACT\n\nUnclassified\n\nNSN 7540-01-280-5500\n\nStandard\n\nIF!I\n\nForm\n\nPrescribed\n298-102\n\nby ANSI\n\n298\nStd,\n\n(Rev.\nZ39-18\n\n2-89)\n\n'
