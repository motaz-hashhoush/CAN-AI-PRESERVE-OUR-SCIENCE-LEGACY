Details concerning the test article general arrangement can be\nfound in Appendix A, Figure 1.\nThe first test configuration was the fully loaded car containing\napproximately 162,000 lbs of coal supported on ASF-11 trucks. The\nwheel/axle sets of the test truck (the same one used for static tests)\nwere supported on slide plate assemblies, two of which were equipped\nwith load cells for measuring vertical forces. These load cells were\npositioned underneath the wheels where the force input was applied.\nThe wheel/axle assemblies of the other truck were supported on spacer\nassemblies which brought the car to a level position. Two hydraulic\nactuators were rigidly coupled to the axles of the test truck for Yaxis testing and actuator forces were reacted by a support fixture rigidly\ncoupled to the test facility floor. A hydraulic power supply was placed\nin the proximity of the actuators. The freight car and truck assemblies\nwere in a standard operational configuration.\nIn the second test configuration, the car was supported the same way\nas the first; however, the actuators were positioned for X-axis testing\nand were rigidly attached to the front axle of the truck assembly. The\nslide plate assemblies with the load cells were positioned beneath the\nwheels with the actuator attachment.\nThe third configuration involved the use of a single actuator positioned at one side of the middle of the car for Z-axis testing. All\nother conditions were the same as test configurations 1 and 2. Each\nof the above test configurations are depicted in Figures 1 through 6\nrespectively.\n3.3 Facilities_ Description - The tests described in this document\nwere conducted at the Martin Marietta Corporation Structures Laboratory\nlocated in Denver, Colorado. All the test equipment used during this \t\ntest was in current calibration throughout the test program except for\n10 charge amplifiers which required an extension beyond 24 March 1975.\n\nj\n\n3\n\n3.3.1 Handling Equipment - Two 100 ton Regent hydraulic jacks and\n50 ton Merrill hydraulic jacks were used to raise the freight car\nand truck assembly during setup operations. A rented conveyor system\nwas used to unload the coal via the hopper hatches which were opened and\nclosed with the aid of a hydraulic jack.\nfour\n\n3.3.2 Fi.xturing - The test truck assembly was\'supported on four MMC\nslide plates and the actuator forces were reacted by support fixtures as\nshown in Appendix B, Figure 7.5.\n3.3.3 Hydraulic System - Forces were applied to the wheel axles by\ntwo hydraulic Moog actuators for the Y and X axis tests and by a single\nactuator for the Z axis tests. The deliverable force rating of an actuator is 14,040 pounds-peak with a 3000 psi differential pressure across\nthe actuator piston. The hydraulic power supply pressure was maintained\nat 3000 psi by a 30 gpm Denison hydraulic pump during testing. An Annin\ncontrol valve was used as a hydraulic oil safety bypass during Z axis\ntesting to preclude the possibility of damage if motion were abruptly\nterminated (see Figure 7).\n3.3.4 Control System - The force input to the test specimen was\nautomatically controlled by a Time Data 1923 digital control system\nwith the master gain located in a remote control console. The control\nsystem was programmed to control the highest of two forces or a single\nforce within a selectable amplitude window. Manual operation was optionally selectable with the use of an analog oscillator in the remote\ncontrol console or the synthesizer used with the digital system. Computer instructions and post-test printout were provided by a remote TTY.\nInput force signals, the drive signal and the servo-valve drive amplifier\nsignals were monitored on a multiplexed scope during testing. Actuator\nphasing (0 0 or 180 0) was accomplished by reversing the output of one of\nthe valve drive amplifiers. The systems described in this section are\nshown in Figures 8 and 9.\n3.3.5 Data Acquisition/Reduction System - The data from each run\nwas recorded on a Honeywell magnetic tape recorder system equipped with\na Vidar 100 channel FM multiplex system. Signal conditioning was accomplished with the use of charge amplifiers, LVDT balance amplifiers, and\nbridge balance amplifiers. Standard accelerometers, displacement transducers, and strain gage transducers were used to measure the motion of\nthe test specimen. A block diagram of the control and data acquisition/\nreduction systems is presented in Appendix B, Figure 7.4. In addition,\nphotographs of these systems are presented in Figures 10 through 13.\n3.4 Test Summary - In order to accomplish the stated objective of\nthe test, it was conducted in accordance with the requirements specified\nir. the test requirements cocument (TS-005-TF, Appendix A). Also presented\nis a listing of controlling force and associated frequency interval for\nthe two actuatox tests along with a lasting of transducer sensitivities.\nQuick look oscillograph data, computer printout, and magnetic tapes generated during the test are available and are being maintained in data\n\nretention files.\n3.4.4 \'?etailed Test Description - The following test descriptions\nprovide information concerning the performance of each major test sequence.\nIn general, control system limitations were experienced during testing\nwhich resulted in premature test run termination (aborts). These aborts\nwere related to fixture resonances, fixture cross coupling and specimen/\nactuator interaction. Typically, a test input spectrum and tolerance\nwas entered into the computer program based on hydraulic system limitations. Data from test attempts and manual excitation at discrete frequencies optimized this spectrum which resulted in maximum input force\nand truck member relative motion. Low level tests preceded maximum input tests to preclude the possibility of over testing the specimen.\nThe above approach produced the desired result below 20 Hz where motion\nwas significant; however, aborts were still experienced above 20 Hz primarily from fixture resonances. These aborts were identified by computer\npost-test printout messages, i.e. "maximum drive limit exceeded" and\n"control limit exceeded".\n\nRuns l through 5 - Precursor tests at three force levels were conducted in the Y-axis with the actuators 180 0 out-of-phase. Insufficient motion was observed during these tests and the following\nconfiguration changes were made. The actuator reaction fixture was\nstiffened by boxing in "I" beam sections and adding additional gussetts. The layer of polychor tape was removed from the bearing\nsurface of the slide plates and molydisulfi.de lubricant was used\nbetween the movable plate which contained polychor tape and the\nbearing plate. Finally, shims were added between the spherical\nbearing end pins of the actuators and the fixture clevis holes.\nRuns 6 through 46 - Force input spectrums were optimized and test\nruns conducted in the Y, X and Z axes. Tests with the actuators\n\ns:.\n\nr\n\n5\n\nboth in and out of phase were conducted in the Y and X axes. Z\naxis tests utilized a single actuator. Maximum relative motion\nof the truck members and the car was achieved while staying within\nthe shaker system capability. Slide plates were inspected and lubricated between the Y and X axes tests. Longitudinal X motion was\nkept to a safe limit to insure that the specimen would remain on\nits supports. Low frequency control limits were set to \xc2\xb18 dB in\norder to accomplish Z axis testing (runs 44 through 46). Previous\ntest limits were generally a maximum of \xc2\xb14 dB. INTRODUCTION\nThis document defines the detailed requirements for conducting\nsinusoidal vibration tests on an 80 ton open hopper railroad freight\ncar. The test program consists of obtaining transfer function data\nfrom vertical and latera: e7citation of the test article for comparison with analytical data.\n1.1 Test ob j ective - The objective of the transfer function test\nis to obtain-data for the validation of the freight car nonlinear elastic model.\n\n2.0 DESCRIPTION OF TEST ARTICLE\nThe test article is an L&N RR model M-042-174 freight car with\nASS\' 11 trucks. Details concerning the test article general arrangement can be found in Figure 1.\n2.1 Test Configuration - The test specimen will be loaded with 80\nton of coal. One truck assembly will be supported by four individual\nlateral slide plates at each wheel location which will be coupled to\nthe test floor through load cells or spacers as required. The other\ntruck assembly will be supported such that the entire assembly is parallel\nwith the facility floor. This test setup is illustratud in Figure 2.\n\nA7\n\nof the General Purpose Laboratory.
