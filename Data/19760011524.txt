The laboratory\nusually affords the best testing environment for a sensor, but the type of target, the conditions of the signal path, and other testing parameters are limited. Because the laboratory environment is usually restrictive, a successful\nlaboratory test _of the sensor is necessary but not sufficient to ensure proper\noperation of the sensor in the real environment. Therefore, much of the testing<is performed in"an environment that duplicates" the conditions under which\nthe instrument is expected to operate. The suc--ess of the testing under such\nconditions requires that the data concerning the environment between the\ninstrument platform and the test site be as accurate as possible.\n\nThis report was produced under Skylab EREP contracts EPN-582, 584,- & 586.\n*Lockheed Electronics Company, Inc.\n\n1\n\nq\nThe magnitude of the effect on the signal caused by interaction between\nthe atmosphere and the signal depends on the type of sensor used, the wavelength employed, and the meteorological conditions prevailing at the time of\nthe measurement.
