Block diagrams and major subsystem component specifications are provided where appropriate.\nINSTRUMENT DESCRIPTION\nNoise Injection Radiometric Technique\n\nA radiometer is a receiver designed to measure the noise power emitted by an\nobject. The level of this received signal is extremely small and is in fact generally smaller than the noise level generated within the receiver. The gain provided\nby the receiver as well as the receiver noise must be extremely stable since fluctuations in either would result in an error in the radiometer measurement. A Dicke\nradiometer is one in which the receiver is alternately switched between the desired\ninput signal and a reference noise source (see fig. 1).\nFollowing filtering and\namplification, a correlation switching operation is performed to down-convert the\ndesired signal from the Dicke frequency to dc. The receiver noise appears as a\ncommon mode component. The result is an effective cancellation of the effects of\nreceiver noise on the measurement accuracy.\nThe noise Injection process establishes the Dicke radiometer as a closed-loop\nfeedback system. Noise is added to the input signal such that the mean value of the\ninput signal plus injected noise is equal to the mean value of the reference noise.\nThis process nearly eliminates the effects of gain fluctuations on the measurement.\nSystem Overview\nA simplified block diagram providing an overview of PBMR is shown in figure 2.\nAn L-band front-end provides the hardware for noise injection, filtering, input Dicke\nmodulation, and amplification.
