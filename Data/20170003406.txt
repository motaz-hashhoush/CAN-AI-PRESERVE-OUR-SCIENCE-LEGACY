Vane4, 1NASA/MSFC Huntsville, AL 35812, steve.creech@nasa.gov, 2Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Pasadena, CA 91109, john.d.baker@jpl.nasa.gov, 3NASA/MSFC Huntsville, AL 35812, angie.jackman@nasa.gov, and 4Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Pasadena, CA 91109, gregg.vane@jpl.nasa.gov\nIntroduction: NASA has successfully completed\nthe Critical Design Review (CDR) of the heavy lift\nSpace Launch System (SLS) and is working towards\nthe first flight of the vehicle in 2018. SLS will begin\nflying crewed missions with an Orion capsule to the\nlunar vicinity every year after the first 2 flights starting\nin the early 2020's. As early as 2021, in addition to\ndelivering an Orion capsule to a cislunar destination,\nSLS will also deliver ancillary payload, termed \xe2\x80\x9cComanifested Payload (CPL)\xe2\x80\x9d, with a mass of at least 5.5\nmT and volume up to 280 m3 simultaneously to that\nsame destination. Later SLS flights have a goal of delivering as much as 10 mT of CPL to cislunar destinations.\nIn addition to cislunar destinations, SLS flights\nmay deliver non-crewed, science-driven missions with\nPrimary Payload (PPL) to more distant destinations.\nSLS PPL missions will utilize a unique payload fairing\noffering payload volume (ranging from 320 m3 to 540\nm3) that greatly exceeds the largest existing Expendable Launch Vehicle (ELV) fairing available. The\nCharacteristic Energy (C3) offered by the SLS system\nwill generate opportunities to deliver up to 40 mT to\ncislunar space, and deliver double PPL mass or decrease flight time by half for some outer planet destinations when compared to existing capabilities. For\nexample, SLS flights may deliver the Europa Clipper\nto a Jovian destination in under 3 years by the mid\n2020\xe2\x80\x99s, compared to the 7+ years cruise time required\nfor current launch capabilities.\nThis presentation will describe ground and flight\naccommodations, interfaces, resources, and performance planned to be made available to potential CPL\nand PPL science users of SLS. In addition, this presentation should promote a dialogue between vehicle developers, potential payload users, and funding sources\nin order to most efficiently evolve required SLS capabilities to meet diverse payload needs as they are identified over the next 35 years and beyond.\n\nNational Aeronautics and Space Administration\n\n5...4...3...2...1...\n\nSPACE LAUNCH SYSTEM\n\nSLS Payload Transportation Beyond LEO\n\nAngie L.
