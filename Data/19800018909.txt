The requirements preaei,_ed .herein are primarily functional in nature,\nbut some detail level requirements are also included.\n\n2.0 ASCENT/DESCENT ANCILLARY DATA\n\nSUPPORT SCOPE\n\nThe A/D ancillary data support for OFT mission evaluation activities shall be\nconfined to providing postflight position, velocity, attitude, and associated\nnavigation and attitude derived parameters for the Orbiter over the flight\nphases and time intervals shown in figure 1. No ancillary data support related\nto the external tank (ET) or the solid rocket boosters (SHB\'s) shall be provided. In addition, the A/D ancillary data products delivered shall be confined\nto those described in section 5.0 of this document.\n\n3.0 DATA PROCESSING APPROACH\n\n(FOR\n\nINFORMATION)\n\nThis section summarizes the A/D ancillary data processing approach in terms of\nthe functional flow diagram shown in figure 2. The approach is presented primarily for information purposes to acquaint prospective ancillary data users with\nthe current data reduction plan. Elements of the approach are subject to change,\npending further analyses of data processing techniques and procedures.\n\n3.1 INPUT DATA\n\nSOURCES\n\nAND INITIAL DATA PRODUCTS (TIER 1)\n\nRaw C- and S-band tracking data shall be obtained from the OFT Mission Control\nCenter (MCC) through the facilities of the Ground Data Systems Division (GDSD).\nSelected onboard navigation and attitude telemetry data shall be obtained from\nthe Institutional Data Systems Division/Data Reduction Complex (IDSD/DRC).\nPhototheodolite data shall not be processed in this task.\n3.2 INTERMEDIATE DATA PRODUCTS (TIER 2)\nPreprocessing or conditioning of the input tracking and telemetry data shall be\naccomplished by using interactive computer graphics techniWes. Ground and\nonboard clock times shall be tabulated to implement correlation of sensor data\nfrom the two input sources. Special event times will be provided by the JSC Instrumentation Integration Branch (WC6).\n\n78FM40\n3.3 FINAL PROCESSING AND DELIVERED OUTPUT DATA PRODUCTS (TIERS 3 1 4 1 AND 5)\nAt the tier-3 level, an ephemeris solution (position, velocity, and acceleration) shall be generated first, using a navigation filter procedure that combines onboard and ground tracking data in the A/D flight regions area. (Both\nsources are available.) In regions where ground tracking is precluded (e.g.,\nduring entry blackout), the solution shall be obtained by using onboard inertial\nmeasurement unit (IMU) data to propagate the state vector computed at the\nterminus of the navigation filter solution. Attitude data are then refined and\ntime-correlated with the overall ephemeris solution. This result shall provide\nthe basis for the delivered output products indicated in tier 4.\nTier-4 processing shall include the computation of the remaining navigation and\nattitude-related parameters needed to complete the parameter set established for\nthe output product; i.e., the product intended to satisfy the ancillary data\nusers. Ascent quick -hook output data will be provided in this tier. The basic\ncomputation for the ascent quick -look outputs is the same as the final outputs\nwith the exception of enhanced BET accuracy. The final descent best estimated\ntrajectory (BET) is developed in tier 4 and reflects the meteorological data\nsupplied to MPAD by WC6.\nMSFC via WC6 will provide the meteorological data for the ascent segment of the\nOFT flights. These data will be provided by MSFC 5 days after flight phase termination. The meteorological data for the descent phase will be supplied by the\nNational Meteorological Center (NMC) 3 weeks after the OFT flight via WC6. The\nNMC requires plots and tabulation of the descent ground track based on telemetry\n(TM) vectors for generating the meteorological data. A time history of centerof-gravity data for the ascent and descent segment of the OFT flights will also\nbe provided by WC6. This information will be reflected in the ascent final and\nquick-look product.\nIn association with the ascent and descent BET\'s, a set (table I(c)) of navigation parameter accuracies will be generated as a special product that gives an\nestimate of the error associated with the tier-3 outputs, which includes the uncertainty associated with the observed meteorological data provided by the MSFC\nand NMC via WC6.\n\n2\n\n1\n\na.\n\n78FM40\n4.0 REQUIRED INPUTS\n\nRaw C- and S-band tracking data shall be obtained from the OFT MCC through the\nfacilities of the GDSD.
