The better the quality of a protein crystal, the more useful it is to\nresearchers\nwho are trying to delineate its structure.\nThe microgravity\nenvironment\nof space allows\nprotein crystals to grow nearly undisturbed\nby convection\nand other gravity-driven\nforces that cause\nflaws to form in them on the ground.\nIn space, lack of convection\nenables protein crystals to grow\nmore slowly than they do on Earth, and the slower a protein crystal grows, the fewer flaws it will have.\nProtein crystal growth experiments\nhave already flown on 14 Space Shuttle missions.\nThis year\'s\nUSML-1 Spacelab\nmission included protein crystal growth experiments\nconducted\nfor commercial\nresearchers.\nThe results of protein crystal experiments\nflown thus far have been larger crystals with\nmore uniform morphologies.\nThe Center for Macromolecular\nCrystallography\nflight hardware\nto meet researchers\'\nneeds and\nexperiments.\n\n(A NASA-cosponsored\nhandles\nsample loading\n\nCCDS) currently\nbuilds\nand retrieval\nfor flight\n\nProtein crystallography\nenables "rational drug design": the development\nof drugs that bind only with\nthe target protein and, hence, do not cause side effects.\nFor example,\npharmaceutical\ncompanies\npresently are interested in developing\ndrugs that can inhibit purine nucleoside\nphosphorylase\n(PNP),\na protein that plays a role in auto-immune\ndiseases.\nTo continue\nthese kinds of investigations,\nresearchers\nneed a constant supply of protein crystals that are as free of flaws as possible.\nSpace Station Freedom will provide the kind of research environment\nthat will enable the production\nof such supplies.\nIn addition, Freedom will provide the kind of long-duration\nfacility required by\nprotein crystal researchers:\n40 percent of proteins require more than two weeks to crystallize.\n\nPRECEDING\n\nPAI_E BLANK\n\nNOT\n\nFILMED\n\n137\n\n\'\n\nProject Organization\nfor\n\nProtein Crystal Growth In
