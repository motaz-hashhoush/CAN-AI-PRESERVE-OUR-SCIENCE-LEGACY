MacDonald\n\nIn May 1986, JSC initiated a cooperative program with the University of Houston - Clear\nLake to support research in computing and information systems. The objective of this program was\nand is to provide continuing long-term research in support of the numerous mission and missionrelated endeavors of NASA/JSC. JSC defined a "cooperative agreement" as the appropriate\ncontractual vehicle to facilitate both .joint participation of researchers from NASA, industry and the\nuniversity community, and sharing of supporting research facilities among the participants. Facilities\nare shared by networking among LJH-CL\'s research and data computing resources and JSCs\ncomputing system.\nA significant part of the cooperative program is its "gateway role." UH-CL is chartered to\ninvolve researchers from outside organizations\nthroughout the US and the world in projects defined\nby professionals at NASA and UH-CL.\nA particularly important set of activities being carried out under the cooperative program is\nin the area of "computer software development."\nBecause it recognized these activities as a critical\nelement of the cooperative program, the Office Aeronautics and Space Technology\nat NASA\nHeadquarters supported the initiation of the Software Engineering\nResearch Center (SERC).\nIn order to meet research and education needs associated with the engineering of large, realtime software systems for NASA\'s future numerous researchers at SERC are investigating:\n\n.\n\n\xc2\xb0\n\n*\n\n4,\n\nengineering reseamh issues central to large distributed systems for real-time and\ndistributed systems with active embedded elements (such as for thespace station)\nconcepts, principles and methodologies\nsoftware systems\n\nfor the engineering\n\nof such large\n\n"computer aided software engineering environments" to advance the state of\nthepractice to achieve improvements in the quality productivity crucial to\nthe application of engineering methodologies to software lifecycle phases\ntheestablishment\nand incorporation\nintooffuture\nsystemsofappropriate\nstandards.\n\nWith the growth of software development\nactivities, comes the increase need for education.\nCurrently most managers and professionals continue to emphasize the implementation and test\nphases of the "software lifecycle." More disciplined engineering approaches require that this\nmanagerial and professional workforce be educated in approaches to software development which\nemphasize requirement and design phases and designing for change.\nQ\n\nThe evolution of engineering\n\nmethodologies\n\nand tools such as CASE and languages\n\nlike\n\nADA over the last ten years has created a severe shortage of individuals who are technically and\nemotionally prepared to exploit these advances.
