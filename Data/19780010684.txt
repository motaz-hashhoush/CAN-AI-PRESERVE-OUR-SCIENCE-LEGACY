operates under the direction of the\nEnvironmental Research Laboratories (ERL) in Boulder, Colorado. The research\nprograms of this laboratory concentrate on two atmospheric phenomena\nof the tropical troposphere: hurricanes and cumulus convection, together\nwith the atmospheric environments in which they occur and with which\nthey interact. These programs include basic research in the description\nof these phenomena and applied research in their prediction and potential\nbeneficial modification. --,.ial observational programs use aircraft\nof the Research Facilities .:enter of ERL to acquire data. A current\nmajor effort is the development of mathematical models through high-speed,\nlareor capacity computers. These models simulate the development,\nstructure, and behavior of hurricanes and cumulus clouds, both in their\nnatural state and when experimentally modified. These studies and models\nare also used to improve prediction capability.\nNHEML is partitioned into four working groups, as shown in Figure\n1. Two important and logical parts of the research programs of NHEML\nare the experimental program in hurricane modification (STOMFURY) and\nthe program in cumulus modification (FACE).
