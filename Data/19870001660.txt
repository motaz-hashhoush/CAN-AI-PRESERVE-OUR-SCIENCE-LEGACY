Morrow\nGoddard Space Flight Center\nGreenbelt, MD 20771\n\nABSTRACT\nFive General Electric (GE) 26.5 All NOAA-G flight nickel-cadmium\ncells were obtained from RCAAstro Electronics\nto undergo performance\ncharacterization\ntesting at the Goddard\nSpace Flight Center\n(GSFC).\nThis lot of cells was manufactured\nwith passivated positive plate, to control nickel structure\nattack during active material impregnation,\nand less electrolyte than normal (<\n3cc/Ah).\nThe cells were\ntested in a parametric\nLow Earth Orbit (LEO) cycling regime that had previously been used to test and\ncharacterize\nGSFC standard 50 Ah cells. Life cycle testing at the Naval Weapons Support Center (NWSC)\nin Crane, Indiana followed.\nThe results of the test showed nominal performance\nin comparison with previous test data on the standard 50. Life cycle testing in the NOAA orbital regime is continuing at NWSC.\n\nINTRODUCTION\nThe NOAA-G spacecraft battery cell flight lot was manufactured by GE in Gainesville, Florida. GE\nfabricated these cells using a new positive electrode processing step designed to increase the strength of the\nelectrode by reducing attack on the nickel sintered structure by active material impregnation solutions.\nThis passivation process reduces the void volume of the cell, thus reducing the amount of electrolyte that\ncan be added.\nQuestions about the reliability of cells with this new processing step and less electrolyte\nbrought about characterization testing by the GSFC.\nCharacterization was accomplished by subjecting 5 cells to a parametric LEO cycling regime developed\nto characterize\nGSFC standard 50 Ah cells. Data was then compared between the cell designs. Following\nthe characterization test, the cells were shipped to NWSC for life cycle evaluation.\n\nTEST\n\nOBJECTIVES\n\nThe objectives of this test program were:\n(1) to study the behavior of aerospace cells manufactured\nwith GE positive\ntrode nickel attack control passivation, (2) to compare this behavior to that of cells without passivation, and (3) to charrize the performance\nof these cells in a typical NOAA orbit with the same charge voltage levels as the NOAA-F spacecraft.\nDescription\nthe cells tested are GE 26.5 Ah aerospace nickel-cadmium\ncells containing third\nI with teflonated\nnegative electrodes, cadmium treated positive electrodes,\nand\ntation.\nThe cells were manufactured\nto GE Manufacturing\nControl Document\nl Catalog Number 42B030ABlO/11.\nThe cells are from NOAA lot 11 activated\nE Acceptance Test Procedure P24A-PB-222 prior to shipment to RCA.\nPerformance\n\nCharacterization\n\nTest\n\n(signal) electrodes.\nThey were manufacpositive electrode nickel attack control\n(MCD) 232A2222AA-80\nand are desigin February 1984 and acceptance tested\n\nDescription\n\n\'he cells were tested in a series connected\n5-cell pack separated and isolated by 3/8-inch aluminum plates followed by\n-inch PVC sheets. This data was plotted against data from the standard 50 Ah cells under the same conditions. The trends of these plots\ncompare very well as can be seen in the sample plots of Figures 5 - 13. Trend data is expected to compare very well for all aerospace nickel-cadmium\ncells regardless of design as is the case here. Poor correlation\nof trend data between cell designs may indicate problems related to\nmanufacture\nand fabrication of the cells.\nData from this test also indicates that the cells exhibit slightly lower voltage characteristics\nthan the 50 Ah cells to which\nthey were compared.\nFull recharge was reached in almost all cases at both voltage levels 5 and 7. In all other\naspects, the cells performed nominally.\nLife Cycle\n\nTest\n\nat NWSC\n\nIn June 1985 following all testing at the GSFC, the cells were sent to NWSC to undergo life cycle testing. The life cycle\nregime is detailed in Figure 14. The cells have performed nominally since the start of testing and have undergone approximately 1200 cycles at the time of this writing. The initial voltage level of 1.47 v/cell has provided approximately\n106% C/D\nas is shown in the typical cycle plots of Figures 15 - 18.\nCONCLUSIONS\nLEO performance\ncharacterization\ntests have shown that the capacity and voltage performance\nof these cells is nominal.\nAlso, parametric test data trend plots show that these cells react in a manner consistent with other aerospace nickel-cadmium\ncells when subjected to various charge-discharge\ncurrents, charge voltage levels, and temperatures.\nFurther, these tests have\nshown that these ceils obtain full recharge with the same range of charge voltage levels as experience predicts.\nParametric tests using the voltage levels and charge-discharge\ncurrents of the NOAA-F spacecraft indicate that these cells\nwill perform satisfactorily under those conditions.\nLastly, life cycle data during the first 1200 cycles of simulated LEO operation shows no abnormal or unexpected\nbehavior.\nIn light of the above findings, the cells should perform nominally in orbit. No problems are foreseen and no concern exists with regard to the low electrolyte\nlevels or the positive plate nickel\nattack control
