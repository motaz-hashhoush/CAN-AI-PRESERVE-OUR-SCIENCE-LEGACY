b'ESDIS Standards O\xef\xac\x83ce (ESO)\nThe ESDIS Standards Office assists the\nESDIS Project in formulating standards\npolicy for NASA Earth Science Data\nSystems (ESDS), coordinates standards\nactivities within ESDIS, and provides\ntechnical expertise and assistance with\nstandards related tasks within the NASA\nEarth Science Data System Working\nGroups (ESDSWG).\n\nRequirements, Standards and Practices\nRequirements\n\nStandards and Practices\n\nAirborne and Satellite Mission Data Systems Requirements\n\n\nESO coordinates standards policy and activities within ESDIS. ESO follows\na three-phase Standards Process for identi\xef\xac\x81cation of appropriate standards\nand practices and their subsequent adoption for use in NASA Earth science\ndata systems.\n\nThe Airborne Mission Data System Requirements and Satellite Mission Data\nRequirements from the NASA HQ Earth Science Division include the ESDIS\nMission Requirements as well as the approved list of standards shown below.\nESDIS Mission Requirements are managed through the ESDIS con\xef\xac\x81guration\ncontrol process involving review and comments from all affected organizations.\nAdditional standards and conventions approved for use in NASA Earth science\ndata systems are managed by the ESDIS Standards Of\xef\xac\x81ce (ESO). Contact the ESO\n(ESDIS Standards Of\xef\xac\x81ce) at eso-staff@lists.nasa.gov and explain the potential\nsubmission. Documents submitted for consideration under the Standards\nProcess are called Request for Comment (RFC) documents.\n\nInitial Screening\n\nESO reviews the RFC, then convenes a Technical Working Group (TWG). The\nTWG reviews the RFC for completeness and to ensure the RFC is ready to be\nreviewed more broadly. This review could lead to more revisions, with assistance\nfrom the ESO.\n\nCommunity Review\n\nCommunity reviews can cover one or both of the following areas: technical\ncontent and operational readiness. Technical content reviews are required only\nfor speci\xef\xac\x81cations that were developed outside of a standards organization. These\nreviews are meant to address whether the speci\xef\xac\x81cation is well-documented and\nis technically implementable. Operational readiness reviews address whether the\nstandard or convention is suitable for operational use and how organizations\nthat have already implemented it have bene\xef\xac\x81ted from it.\nThe TWG analyzes community responses and makes a recommendation to\nESDIS whether to approve the RFC. If the recommendation of the TWG to ESDIS\nis positive, and if ESDIS concurs, then the RFC is approved and is posted on list\nof Standards and References in the ESO section of the Earthdata web site.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nRelated Technical Information\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nDocument Submission\n\n\nDiscovery and Access\nThe Data Access Protocol - DAP 2.0\nOpenGIS \xc2\xae Web Map Service Version 1.1.1\nCEOS OpenSearch\n\nData Format\n\nStandards Process\nIndividuals and organizations are encouraged to contact the ESO with\nsuggestions for existing or developing standards and practices.
