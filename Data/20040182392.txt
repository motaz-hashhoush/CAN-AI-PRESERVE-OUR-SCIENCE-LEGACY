ABSTRACT\n\nThe existing U.S. hub-and-spoke air transportation system is reaching saturation. Major aspects of the current system, such as\ncapacity, safety, mobility, customer satisfaction, security, communications, and ecological effects, require improvements. The\nchanging dynamics - increased presence of general aviation, unmanned autonomous vehicles, military aircraft in civil airspace\nas part of homeland defense - contributes to growing complexity of airspace. The system has proven remarkably resistant to\nchange. NASA Langley Research Center and the National Institute of Aerospace conducted a workshop on Transportation\nNetwork Topologies on 9-10 December 2003 in Williamsburg, Virginia. The workshop aimed to examine the feasibility of\ntraditional methods for complex system analysis and design as well as potential novel alternatives in application to\ntransportation systems, identify state-of-the-art models and methods, conduct gap analysis, and thus to lay a foundation for\nestablishing a focused research program in complex systems applied to air transportation.\n15.
