ABSTRACT(Maximum200 words)\n\nFor Earth-observing\nsatellite instruments,\nit was standard to consider each instrument band to have a spectral response that\nis infinitely narrow, i.e., to have a response from a single wavelength.\nThe SeaWiFS bands, however, have nominal spectral\nbandwidths\nof 20 and 40 nm. These bandwidths effect the SeaWiFS measurements\non orbit. The effects are also linked to\nthe manner in which the instrument was calibrated and to the spectral shape of the radiance that SeaWiFS views. The\nspectral shape of that radiance will not be well known on orbit. In this technical memorandum,\ntwo source spectra are\nexamined. The first is a 12,000 K Planck function, and the second is based on the modeling results of H. Gordon at the\nUniversity of Miami. By comparing these spectra, the best available corrections to the SeaWiFS measurements\nfor source\nspectral shape, plus estimates of the uncertainties\nin these corrections,\ncan be tabulated.\n\n14, SUBJECTTERMS\nSeaWiFS, Oceanography,\nSpectral Shape, Radiance\nSolar Diffuser\n17.
