The intensity and mean energy of\nauroral electrons is correlated suggesting that the fluctuation in\nauroral intensity comes primarily from the variability of the\nacceleration mechanism rather than the variations in the particle\npopulation densities.\n\nThe results were published in a number of\n\noral presentations and in scientific journals.\n\nDuring the 1969\n\nCV990 expedition, coordinated data was taken with the Lockheed\nparticle experiments on OV1-18 and ATS-5 satellites. The satellite\ndata was reduced to facilitate coordinated investigations with the\nairborne experimenters. The airborne photometric X5200 NI data\nwere analyzed and the effective life time was found to be 4.5\nminutes.\n\n-1-\n\nOBJECTIVE\nTo provide a final summary of the work accomplished in the\nprogram entitled "Satellite Data Analysis" under Contract Number\nNAS2-6299.\n\nINTRODUCTION\nIn the framework of a previous program entitled "6 Channel\nPhotometric Observations from the CV990 Aircraft" (NASw 1997),\nLockheed instrumentation and scientific personnel took part in\nthe 1969 NASA CV990 auroral aircraft expedition.\n\nThe data taken during this expedition was combined with the\ndata taken during the 1968 expedition and was analyzed during the\npresent program.\n\nThe data obtained during the two expeditions constitutes an\nextremely fine body of absolute photometric observations of the\naurora.\n\nNo comparable body of data having such extensive coverage,\n\nhigh sensitivity, and high resolution exists.\n\nThe absolute\n\ncalibration permits the direct interpretation of the measurements.\nThe regularity and frequency of the observations permits the use\nof statistical methods.\n\nThe zenithal total column intensity\n\nmeasurements can be very simply interpreted as absolute particle\nintensities.\n\nThe high altitude aircraft platform permitted the\nThe uncertainties normally intro-\n\nspatial scanning of the aurora.\n\nduced by lower atmospheric scattering and absorption are minimized\nby the high altitude aircraft platform.\n\nThe analysis of the data did produce some very significant\nresults in the understanding of the auroras.\n\nFurther analysis\n\nof this type of data still promises great returns and there are\na large number of questions which could be answered by the more\ndetailed analysis of these data.\n\n-2-\n\nThere have been a number of observations during the 1969 aircraft expedition which were coordinated with the Lockheed experiments\non OV1-18 and ATS-5 satellites.\n\nOne of the purposes of this contract\n\nwas to reduce the satellite data into a form which is amenable for\nanalysis and comparison with the airborne data.\n\nACCOMPLISHMENTS\nNew Results From Photometric Data\nThe Lockheed airborne data was analyzed in more detail and a\nnumber of new discoveries were made during the period of this contract as regards to the properties of the aurora.\n1.\n\nThe dayside soft-zone is associated with the dayside cusp region\nand the particles are soft being of magnetosheath energies\n(Eather and Mende, 1971a).\n\n2.\n\nIn the nightside auroral zone, two types of electron auroras\noccurred; namely, the hard aurora generated by electrons in the\nkeV range and the softer aurora, often subvisual, generated by\nelectrons in the 100-1000 eV range (Eather and Mende, 1971b).\n\n3.\n\nThe occurrence of soft auroras are very widespread and occur continuously within the nightside of the oval.\nless frequently.\n\nThe hard types occur\n\nThe nightside soft zone at higher latitude is\n\ncaused by the absence of the hard components (Eather and Mende,\n1971c).\n4.\n\nThe soft precipitation is related to the plasma sheet, both in\nmean energy and flux of electrons (Eather and Mende, 1971b).\n\n5.\n\nThe intensity of auroras are correlated with mean energy.\n\nThe\n\nratio of 6300 and 4278 intensities decreases with increasing 4278\nintensity.\n\nThe 5577 to 4278 intensity ratio also follows this\n\nsame tendency, but to a lesser extent (Eather and Mende, 1971c).\n6.\n\nThe mean electron energy at precipitation fluctuates very largely\nin the aurora while the number flux is relatively constant (Eather\nand Mende, 1971b,c).\n\n7.\n\nA technique was developed in which a rough measurement of auroral\nprimary parameters could be obtained from ground based observations.\n\nThese parameters are:\n\n-3-\n\nproton flux, electron energy flux,\n\nmean electron energy parameter.\n\nThis provides a technique of\n\nmonitoring these quantities without the use of a spacecraft\n(Eather and Mende, 1971c).\n\nReduction of Satellite Data\nThe satellite data were reduced to absolute calibrated partikcIe\nfluxes for the coordination periods listed.\n\nThe satellite data are\n\nincluded in this Quantitative\ncomparisons can be made so that the absolute efficiencies generating\nthe visible and infrared emissions could be evaluated.\nThe Lockheed photometric data should be analyzed further and\nsubstorm times introduced into the data.\n\nAll the data analysis so\n\nfar took large averages regardless of the status of the magnetosphere.\n\nThe substorm occurrence times should be established for all\n\nflights from world wide magnetograms.\n\nThis then has to be intro-\n\nduced into the statistical analysis.\nThe conclusions of the airborne photometer experiment are summarized in the review paper entitled "High Latitude Particle Precipitation and Source Regions in the Magnetosphere."
