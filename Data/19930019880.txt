ABSTRACT\n\nCenter, Hampton, VA;\n\n- 39\n\n(Maximum\n\n200 words)\n\nThe corrosion fatigue crack growth characteristics of small surface and comer cracks in aluminum alloy 2024 is\nestabllished.\nThe damaging effect of salt water on the early stages of small crack growth is characterized by:\n1. crack initiation at constituent particle pits, 2. intergranular\nmicrocracking for a < 100 wn, and 3. transgranular\nsmall crack growth for a <100 I_m. In aqueous 1% NaC1 and at a constant anoclic potential of -700 mVSCE, small\ncracks exhibit a factor of three increase in fatigue crack growth rates compared to laboratory air. Small cracks\nexhibit accelerated corrosion fatigue crack growth rates at low levels of &K (<1 MPa_/m) below long crack &Kth.\nWhen exposed to Paris regime levels of crack tip stress intensity, small corrosion fatigue cracks exhibit growth rates similar\nto that observed for long cracks. Results suggest that crack closure effects influence the corrosion fatigue crack growth\nrates of small cracks (a__100I_m). This is evidenced by similar small and long crack growth behavior at various levels of R.\nContrary to the corrosion fatigue characteristics of small cracks in high strength steels, no pronounced chemical crack\nlength effect is observed for alloy 2024 exposed to salt water.\n\n14.\n\nSUBJECT TERMS\n\n15.
