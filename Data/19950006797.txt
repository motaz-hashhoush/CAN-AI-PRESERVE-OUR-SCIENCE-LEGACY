Gilson\nila\nLincoln Laboratory, Massachusetts Institute of Technology\n244 wo street\nod\nLexington, Massachusetts 02173-9108\n\nABSTRACT\nA series of multi-frequency radar measurements of aircraft wakes at altitudes of 5,000 to 25,000 ft. was\nperformed at Kwajalein, R.M.I., in May and June of 1990. Two aircraft were tested, a Learjet 35 and a\nLockheed C-5A. The cross-section of the wake of the Learjet was too small for detection at Kwajalein.\nThe wake of the C-5A, although also very small, was detected and measured at VHF,UHF, L-, S-, and\nC-bands, at distances behind the aircraft ranging from about one hundred meters to tens of kilometers. The\ndata suggest that the mechanism by which aircraft wakes have detectable radar signatures is, contrary to\nprevious expectations, unrelated to engine exhaust but instead due to turbulent mixing by the wake vortices\nof pre-existing index of refraction gradients in the ambient atmosphere. These measurements were of\nnecessity performed with extremely powerful and sensitive instrumentation radars, and the wake crosssection is too small for most practical applications.\n\n, This\n\nwork was sponsored by the Office of Naval Technology.
