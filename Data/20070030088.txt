Administration\n\nDebris Removal Categories\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nDebris removal concepts are often categorized by\n\xe2\x80\x93 The size of the debris to be removed: typically < or > 10 cm\n(statistical or designated removal)\n\xe2\x80\x93 The altitude regime of removal: LEO, MEO, or GEO\n\xe2\x80\x93 The basing of the removal device: ground-based, air-based, or space-based\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nRemoval of small debris normally affects the near-term environment by\nreducing collision probabilities for existing space systems, e.g., cleansing\nhuman space flight altitude regimes.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nRemoval of large debris influences the mid-term and far-term environments\nby reducing the number of debris-generating collisions.\n\n11\n\nNational Aeronautics and Space operational).\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nDeployment and operations costs can be very high, particularly for spacebased systems.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nSource of funding (government or commercial) is unclear.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nExisting treaties and conventions might inhibit the removal of large debris.\n\xe2\x80\x93 Owner\xe2\x80\x99s consent\n\xe2\x80\x93 Liability issues\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nUncontrolled reentry of large debris poses additional risks of human\ncasualty and property damage.\n\n13\n\nNational Aeronautics and Space Administration\n\nSummary\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nSpace debris mitigation practices will be insufficient to prevent the\ncontinued growth of the Earth satellite population.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nRemoval of orbital debris can improve the reliability of present and future\nspace systems.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nThe challenges of developing an effective, affordable debris removal\ncapability are considerable.\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nThe time is right for a new look at space remediation concepts.\n\xe2\x80\x93 In concert with or following the current IAA study\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2\n\nAn international approach to the remediation of the near-Earth space\nenvironment will likely be required.\n\n14\n\n'
