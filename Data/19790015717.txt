Abstract\nCharacteristics of an Advanced Supersonic Technology reference configuration\ndesignated AST-I05-1 have been generated as part of a continuing technology development and assessment activity. This detailed systems integration study supports\nthe Langley Research Center noise trade-off stUdy (1978) performed for the FAA/ICAO\nby giving credence to systems weights and assurance that the noise study ASTconcept can be balanced. Current titanium structural technology is assumed. The\nAST-I05-1 is the first of several AST\'s to be configured with an aircraft propulsion\ncompany engine concept; a Pratt and Whitney Aircraft duct-burning turbofan variable\nstream control engine (VSCE), with noise reduction potential through use of a\ncoannular nozzle. With 273 passengers, range of the AST-I05-1 for a cruise Mach\nnumber of 2.62 is essentially transpacific. Lift-to-drag ratio is slightly higher than\nfor previous AST configurations. It is trimmable over a center-of-gravity range of\n4.7m (15.5 ft). Inherent high positive effective dihedral, typical of arrow-wing configurations in high-lift approach, would limit AST-I05-1 to operating in crosswinds of\n11.6 m/sec (22.4 kt), or less, with 75 percent of available lateral control. Normal\npower takeoff with cutback results in noise in excess of Federal Aviation Regulation\nPart 36 but less than for conventional procedure takeoff.
