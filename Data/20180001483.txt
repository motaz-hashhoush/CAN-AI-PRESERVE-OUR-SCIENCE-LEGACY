and NASA unique\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 PICA and HEEET\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Simple compositional systems and manufacturing is automated\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Both are essentially carbon and phenolic composition\n\xe2\x80\xa2 PICA uses rayon. HEEET does not (by choice)\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Technology capability has been transferred to industry\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Raw material and processing \xe2\x80\x93 off the shelf \xe2\x80\x93 need to keep an eye\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Multiple vendors and processing involved\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Raw material to processing/manufacturing to assembly and integration\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 NASA has to assess the risk of atrophy periodically\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Approaches to addressing capability availability in the future\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Periodically assess and take risk mitigation action for sustaining the\ncapability\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 HEEET and PICA are NASA IP and NASA can transfer the technology at any time\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Build and store a common aeroshell design capable of missions at multiple\ndestinations\n2/23/2018\n\nOPAG, Feb 23, Technology Forum, Hampton, VA\nEthiraj.Venkatapathy-1@nasa.gov\n\n12\n\nCommon Probe Study\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Could a common atmospheric probe design be used for missions to Venus, Jupiter,\nSaturn, Uranus, and Neptune?
