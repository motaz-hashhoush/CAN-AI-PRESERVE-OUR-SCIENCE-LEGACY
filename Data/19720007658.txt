R. D. Sharp and R. G. Johnson, and a paper entitled "Coordinated\nAuroral Electron Observations from a Synchronous and a Polar Satellite,\nby R. D. Sharp, D. L. Carr, R. G. Johnson, and E. G. Shelley, was accepted\nfor publication by the Journal of Geophysical Research.\n\nSubstantial progress\n\nin the analysis of the data has been achieved in several other areas as\ndescribed below.\n\nDISCUSSION\nThe detailed analysis of the substorm at 0530 on Day 44, 1970 has been\nlargely completed and a paper is in the course of preparation (see Seventh\nQuarterly Progress Report). Besides members of our group, co-authors on\nthe paper will include:\n\nDr. Steven Mende, Lockheed Palo Alto Research\n\nLaboratory, who provided the all-sky camera data; Dr. Gerhard Haerendel\nof the Max-Planck-Institute of Extraterrestrische Physik in Munich, who\nanalyzed the current systems; and Dr. Edward Hones of Los Alamos Scientific\nLaboratory, who provided the VELA data in the magnetotail. Some of the\ndata from this substorm event are shown in Figures 1 through 10.\nWork is currently in progress in four other major areas.\n1)\n\nDr. Shelley is continuing his studies of the average properties\n\nof the energetic plasma at synchronous altitude. A previous analysis of\na limited body of data from an engineering standpoint was presented by\n-1-\n\nE. G. Shelley and S. K. Lew at the National Symposium on Natural and Man-Made\nRadiation in Space, 2-5 March 1971 at Las Vegas, Nevada. The present effort,\nin addition to increasing the quantity of data in the study, will emphasize\nthe physical significance of the various systematics and regularities that\nhave been discovered.\n2) Dr. Sharp is making a study of the temporal variations of the\nplasma properties during three contrasting periods of magnetic activity,\neach of about five days duration. They include one quiet period, the magnetic\nstorm period of 29 September-2 October 1969, and one non-storm period of substantial activity. The ATS-5 in-situ measurements of the ring current are\ncompared with variations in the Dst index; and substorm variations in the\nelectron and proton fluxes are compared to auroral zone magnetometer data\nfrom stations near the foot of the ATS-5 field line.\n3) A statistical study of the plasma variations during a number of\nsubstorms has been initiated by Mr. D. L. Carr. He is using as a fiducial\nmark the characteristic signature in the energetic (E > 38 keV) protons which\nhas been found to be indicative of both the time and the place of the initiation of the magnetospheric collapse (see Third Quarterly Progress Report,\nPage 12). This study will investigate whether there are a critical set of\nplasma conditions which are necessary to initiate this substorm instability.\n4) A follow-on to the coordinated ATS-5/OVl-18 study of simultaneous\nelectron measurements (J. Geophys. Res., in press, see Sixth Quarterly\nProgress Report) is being undertaken by Mr. L. F. Smith. This will be a\nsimilar comparison of the simultaneous auroral proton measurements from\nthe two satellites.\n\nRECOMMENDATIONS\nThe above-described projects are representative of a large number of\ninvestigations of the data from the Lockheed experiment which could profitably be performed. Each of them could substantially further out knowledge\nof the physics of the magnetosphere. We urge that data analysis funding\nnot be terminated at this point. The large investment made in acquiring\n-2-\n\nthe data and developing the computer programs necessary to process it is\njust at the point of substantial payoff and it would be extremely unfortunate\nto stop work now.\n\nR.
