ABSTRACT\n\n(Ma.x_rrcrn200\n\nDISTRIBUTION\n\n1\n\nCODE\n\nwords)\n\nThis document provides brief reports, or case studies, on a number of investigations\nsponsored by the Calibration\nand\nValidation\nTeam (CVT) within the Sea-viewing\nWide Field-of-view\nSensor (SeaWiFS) Project. Chapter 1 describes the\ncalibration\nand characterization\nof the GSFC sphere, which was used in the recent recalibration\nof the SeaWiFS instrument.\nChapter 2 presents a revision of the diffuse attenuation coefficient, K(490), algorithm based on the SeaWiFS wavelengths.\nChapter 3 provides an implementation\nscheme for an algorithm to remove out-of-band\nradiance when using a sensor\ncalibration based on a finite width (truncated) spectral response function, e.g., between the 1% transmission\npoints. Chapter\n4 describes the implementation\nschemes for the stray light quality flag (local area coverage [LAC] and global area coverage\n[GAC]) and the LAC stray light correction.\n\n14, SUBJECTTERMS\nSeaWiFS, Oceanography,\n\nCalibration,\n\nValidation,\n\nDiffuse Attenuation\nCoefficient,\nK(490),\nStray Light Correction Algorithm\n17.
