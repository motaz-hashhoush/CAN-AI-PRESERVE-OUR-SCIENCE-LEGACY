Pressure Relief Valve (PPRV) as part of the Advanced Extravehicular Mobility Unit (EMU)\n(AEMU) Portable Life Support System (PLSS). The RV-346 serves as the Positive Pressure Relief Valve (PPRV),\nand the RV-348 serves as the Secondary Positive Pressure Relief Valve (SPPRV).\n1.2\n1.2.1\n\nCONVENTIONS AND NOTATIONS\nRATIONALE\n\nA rationale statement is included for each requirement. The rationale statement indicates why the requirement is\nneeded, shows the basis for its inclusion in this requirements document, and provides context and examples to\nstakeholders. It is important to note that rationale is not binding, and it only provides supporting information. In the\nevent that there is an inconsistency between the requirement and the rationale, the requirement is binding and takes\nprecedence. If there is any confusion between the requirement and rationale, seek further guidance from the\nresponsible technical authority.\n\n1.2.2\n\nBACKGROUND\n\nPPRV development for the advanced PLSS began in 2012 with an initial prototype set (4 units) was developed by\nAirlock via Oceaneering Space Systems (OSS) in accordance with Rev A of this specification. The valve design\nconsidered ISS EMU Item 146 valve failures including issues with stiction and modeled the initial valve design from\nthe Bends Treatment Adapter (BTA) relief valve used on the ISS EMU Program which had performed well over the\nduration of the program without issues from stiction. In 2014, after Airlock developed the updated PPRV design for\nISS EMU Item 146 and delivered the valve, OSS/Airlock developed the PPRV 2.0 valve design based on that work\nand in compliance with Revision B of this specification delivering two units. To date, the PPRV 2.0 has suffered\nfrom high crack due to stiction with very little dwell time. The next iteration of the valve will comply with the most\nrecent version of this specification and focus on eliminating stiction as the valve must be capable of sitting dormant\nwithout failing to meet functional requirements.\n36B\n\nFigure 1-1\xe2\x80\x93 PPRV 1.0 to ISS EMU Prototype Comparison\n\n7\n\nDevelopment Specification for Positive Pressure Relief Valve It is also the means of relieving and controlling suit ventilation\nloop pressure during a controlled vacuum chamber or airlock depress. It is not intended to provide relief for rapidly\noccurring external events such as a rapid cabin depressurization scenario; doing so would drive the reaction time and\nover-drive the valve size.\n\nFigure 3-1 - PPRV Functional Schematic\n3.2\n3.2.1\n\nLIFE\n[R.PPRV.346.001] OPERATIONAL LIFE\n\nThe PPRV shall have operating life as specified in Error! The details of what each launch/landing cycle entails depends on the vehicle\nchosen, but is encompassed by the requirements provided in the environments section.\n3.3\n3.3.1\n\nOPERATING FLUIDS\n[R.PPRV.346.006] GASEOUS NITROGEN\n\nThe PPRV shall be compatible with and operate using gaseous nitrogen per MIL-PRF-27401, Performance\nSpecification for Propellant Pressurizing Agent, Nitrogen, Type I, Grade B as a test fluid.\nRationale: Gaseous nitrogen provides a safe and effective method for performing development tests before oxygen\ncompatibility testing and approval. It will be the gas used for a variety of PPRV and PLSS-level tests.\n3.3.2\n\n[R.PPRV.346.007] GASEOUS OXYGEN\n\nThe PPRV shall be compatible with and operate using gaseous oxygen per MIL-PRF-27210, Performance\nSpecification for Oxygen, Aviators Breathing, Liquid and Gas, Type I as the working fluid.\nRationale: Oxygen is the required EVA operating fluid. To fulfill this function, all wetted components must be oxygencompatible and cleaned for oxygen use.\n\n10\n\nDevelopment Specification for Positive Pressure Relief Valve (PPRV)\n\n3.4\n3.4.1\n\nCTSD-CX-0070C\n\nOPERATION AND PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS\n[R.PPRV.346.008] CRACKING PRESSURE\n\nThe PPRV shall crack at a pressure of 59.3 \xe2\x80\x93 60.7 kPa differential [8.6 - 8.8 psid] in all possible orientations.\nRationale:\n(1) Cracking pressure is defined as the pressure which produces a flow of > .05 lb/hr. The crack pressure is\nbased on the CxSuit Purge Valve-Regulator-PPRV sizing analysis 10/24/09.\n[R.PPRV.346.009] RESEAT PRESSURE\n\n3.4.2\n\nThe PPRV shall reseat at a pressure of 59.3 \xe2\x80\x93 60.7 kPa differential [8.6 - 8.8 psid] in all possible orientations.\nRationale:\n(1) Reseat pressure is defined as the differential pressure where the valve as it approaches closure reduces the\nflow to < .05 lb/hr at a temperature of 21.1\xc2\xb0C [70\xc2\xb0F]. The reseat pressure is based on the CxSuit Purge\nValve-Regulator-PPRV sizing analysis 10/24/09.\n[R.PPRV.346.010] FULL OPEN FLOW\n\n3.4.3\n\nThe PPRV shall flow a minimum of 3.4 kg/hr [7.49 lbm/hr] dry O2 at 21.1\xc2\xb0C [70\xc2\xb0F] with a suit pressure of 69.6 kPa\n[10.1 psia] and vacuum as the external reference.\n416B\n\nRationale:\n(1) This is required to tolerate a failed open Primary Oxygen Regulator or Secondary Oxygen Regulator.\n(2) Vacuum can be roughing quality at < 20 torr.\n796B\n\n790B\n\n791B\n\n3.4.4\n\n[R.PPRV.346.011] FAILED OPEN FLOW\n\nThe PPRV, under a failed open condition, shall flow a maximum of 1.54 kg/hr [3.4 lbs/hr] dry O2 at 4.4\xc2\xb0C [40\xc2\xb0F]\nwith a suit pressure of 23.4 kPa [3.4 psia] with the flow direction perpendicular to the direction of the force of gravity.\nRationale:\n(1) This is required for the Primary Oxygen Regulator and Secondary Oxygen Regulator to maintain a habitable\nsuit pressure with a failed open Positive Pressure Relief Valve (PPRV).\n(2) Direction definition based on the fact that a poppet\xe2\x80\x99s weight will affect flow magnitude if oriented towards\nor away from a gravity field.\n(3) This requirement is based on a vacuum reference condition, hence the \xe2\x80\x9cpsia\xe2\x80\x9d units. This will ensure that\nthe relief valve meets requirements under EVA conditions.
