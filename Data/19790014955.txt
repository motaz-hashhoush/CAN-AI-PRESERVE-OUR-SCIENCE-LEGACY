Further, the sensing systems\nmust satisfy these requirements in a cost-effective\nmanner that takes\nmaximum\nadvantage of cormaon\nequipment and economiesof scale. This\nstudy provides a first step toward that end by defining generic mission\nconcepts that satisfy a broad spectrum of earth resources and environmental mission objectives.\nThe intent of this study is to generate mission specifications\nfor three generic mission classes to provide a baseline for definition\nand analysis of Data Acquisition Platform (DAP) system concepts. The\nmission specifications define compatible groupings of sensors that satisfy\nspecific earth resources and environmental mission objectives.\nThe\ndriving force behind the definition of sensor groupings is mission need;\nplatform and STSconstraints are of secondary importance.\nThe three generic mission classes are: (I) low earth orbit\nsun-synchronous; (2) geosynchronous; and (3) non-sun-synchronous, nongeosynchronous. These missions are chosen to provide a variety of\nsensor complementsand implementation concepts. Each mission specification relates mission categories, mission objectives, measured parameters,\nand candidate sensors to orbits and coverage, operations compatibility,\nand platform fleet size. The mission definitions developed in this\nstudy are undoubtedly subject to modification, revision, and updating,\nbut at this point in time they establish a documentedbaseline for\nfuture mission and systems definition activities.\n\nOBJECTIVE\nANDAPPROACH\nOBJECTIVE/DELIVERABLES\n- Three missions\nare defined\nand documented\na Final Report.\nAn Implementation\nPlan was submitted\nin the first\nweek of the study and is included\nas Appendix\nC.
